CVII. ad Lesbiam

Si quicquam cupido optantique optigit umquam
     insperanti, hoc est gratum animo proprie.
quare hoc est gratum nobis quoque carius auro
     quod te restituis, Lesbia, mi cupido.
restituis cupido atque insperanti, ipsa refers te
     nobis. o lucem candidiore nota!
quis me uno vivit felicior aut magis hac est
     optandus vita dicere quis poterit?

If anything loving and wished for ever happens to you out of the blue,
  it is genuinely pleasing to your soul.
Therefore it is also pleasing and dearer then gold to us
  that you, Lesbia, return to me, who loves you.
You return to a loving man who didn't see it coming, you bear yourself back
  to us. O light with a brighter sign!
Who lives more happily than me alone, or what loving man
  can name a life that is better than this?
