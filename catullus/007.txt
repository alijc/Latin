VII. ad Lesbiam

Quaeris, quot mihi basiationes
tuae, Lesbia, sint satis superque.
quam magnus numerus Libyssae harenae
lasarpiciferis iacet Cyrenis
oraclum Iovis inter aestuosi
et Batti veteris sacrum sepulcrum;
aut quam sidera multa, cum tacet nox,
furtivos hominum vident amores:
tam te basia multa basiare
vesano satis et super Catullo est,
quae nec pernumerare curiosi
possint nec mala fascinare lingua. 


To Lesbia

You ask, how many to me your kisses,
Lesbia, would be enough and over.

As a great number of the grains of African sand
lasarpiciferis situated at Cyrenae
the oracle of Jupiter among the sacred tombs
of sultry and ancient Battus;

or many stars, when night is still,
see the secret loves of men:

to give that many kisses to you 
is enough and more to mad Catullus,

that spies could neither count them up
nor cast a spell with a wicked tongue.
