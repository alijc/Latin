XII. ad Matrucinum Asinium

Marrucine Asini, manu sinistra
non belle uteris: in ioco atque vino
tollis lintea neglegentiorum.
hoc salsum esse putas? fugit te, inepte:
quamvis sordida res et invenusta est.
non credis mihi? crede Pollioni
fratri, qui tua furta vel talento
mutari velit: est enim leporum
differtus puer ac facetiarum.
quare aut hendecasyllabos trecentos
exspecta, aut mihi linteum remitte,
quod me non movet aestimatione,
verum est mnemosynum mei sodalis.
nam sudaria Saetaba ex Hiberis
miserunt mihi muneri Fabullus
et Veranius: haec amem necesse est
ut Veraniolum meum et Fabullum. 

XII. to Matrucinus Asinius

Matrucinus Asinius, you don't use your
left hand well: in jest and in wine
you steal the napkins of careless men.
Do you think this is funny? It shuns you, fool:
however much the business is sordid and unlovely.
You don't believe me? Believe your brother
Pollionus, who would rather your tricks
be exchanged even for money: for it is 
a boy stuffed with humor and wit.
And so either watch out for three hundred
lines of verse, or send back my napkin to me,
because it doesn't move me by its value,
in truth, it is a souvenir of my companion.
For the Saetaban hankerchiefs from Hiberii
Fabullus and Veranius sent bribes to me;
I would love these things  it is necessary
as my Veraniolum and Fabullum.
