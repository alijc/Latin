XIII. ad Fabullum

Cenabis bene, mi Fabulle, apud me
paucis, si tibi di favent, diebus,
si tecum attuleris bonam atque magnam
cenam, non sine candida puella
et vino et sale et omnibus cachinnis.
haec si, inquam, attuleris, venuste noster,
cenabis bene; nam tui Catulli
plenus sacculus est aranearum.
sed contra accipies meros amores
seu quid suavius elegantiusve est:
nam unguentum dabo, quod meae puellae
donarunt Veneres Cupidinesque,
quod tu cum olfacies, deos rogabis,
totum ut te faciant, Fabulle, nasum.

XIII. to Fabullus

You'll dine well, my Fabullus, with me
in a few days' time, if the gods favor you,
if you bring with you a fine and abundant
dinner, not excluding a fair lass
and wine and wit and lots of loud laughter.
If you bring these, I say, my charmer,
you will dine well; for your Catullus'
purse is full of spider-webs.
But you'll get pure love instead
or what is sweeter or more tasteful:
for I'll give you ointment, that my lass
got from Venuses and Cupids,
that when you smell it, you'll pray to the gods
that they make you, Fabullus, all nose.
