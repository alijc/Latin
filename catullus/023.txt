XXIII. ad Furium 

Furi cui neque servus est neque arca
nec cimex neque araneus neque ignis,
verum est et pater et noverca, quorum
dentes vel silicem comesse possunt,
est pulcre tibi cum tuo parente
et cum coniuge lignea parentis.
nec mirum: bene nam valetis omnes,
pulcre concoquitis, nihil timetis,
non incendia, non graves rvinas,
non facta impia, non dolos veneni,
non casus alios periculorum.
atque corpora sicciora cornu
aut siquid magis aridum est habetis
sole et frigore et esuritione.

To Furius, to whom is neither a servant/slave or chest
or bed-bug or spider or fire,
in truth/it's true both father and stepmother, of whom
they can eat teeth or pebbles,
it is finely to you with your parent
and with the woody spouse of your parent.
And not strange: for you all fare well,
you digest finely, you fear nothing,
neither fires, nor serious catastrophes,
or wicked deeds, or the treachery of poison,
or other fortunes of danger.
And your bodies, dry? as a horn
or if anything more dry it is you have
by sun and cold and hunger.
