XLII. ad hendecasyllabos

Adeste, hendecasyllabi, quot estis
omnes undique, quotquot estis omnes.
iocum me putat esse moecha turpis,
et negat mihi nostra reddituram
pugillaria, si pati potestis.
persequamur eam et reflagitemus.
quae sit, quaeritis? illa, quam videtis
turpe incedere, mimice ac moleste
ridentem catuli ore Gallicani.
circumsistite eam, et reflagitate,
'moecha putida, redde codicillos,
redde putida moecha, codicillos!'
non assis facis? o lutum, lupanar,
aut si perditius potes quid esse.
sed non est tamen hoc satis putandum.
quod si non aliud potest ruborem
ferreo canis exprimamus ore.
conclamate iterum altiore voce.
'moecha putide, redde codicillos,
redde, putida moecha, codicillos!'
sed nil proficimus, nihil movetur.
mutanda est ratio modusque vobis,
siquid proficere amplius potestis:
'pudica et proba, redde codicillos.'

XLII. to eleven-syllable verses

Come, my lines of satire, as many as you are
every one from every side, however many you all are.
The filthy slut thinks me a joke,
and refuses to return your writing 
tablets to me, if you can bear it.
Let's chase her down and demand them back.
Who is it you're looking for? That one that you see
repulsively strutting, farcically and irksomely
laughing with a Gallic pup's mouth.
Gather round her, and demand them back,
"you stinking whore, return my notebooks,
return, you stinking whore, my notebooks."
Aren't you making pennies?  O muck, brothel,
or anything that you could be that's worse.
But this is still not enough for the reckoning.
But if no one else can, let's force out the 
dog's blush with an iron mouth.
Cry out again in a louder voice.
'you stinking whore, return my notebooks,
return, you stinking whore, my notebooks?'
But we accomplish nothing, nothing is disturbed.
The reckoning ought to be moved and the manner to you,
If you can accomplish what is greater:
"virtuous and honorable woman, return my notebooks."
