
XLVII. ad Porcium et Socrationem

Porci et Socration, duae sinistrae
Pisonis, scabies famesque mundi,
vos Veraniolo meo et Fabullo
verpus praeposuit Priapus ille?
vos convivia lauta sumptuose
de die facitis, mei sodales
quaerunt in triuio vocationes?


XLVII. to Porcius and Socration

Porcius and Socration, two left hands
of Piso, manges and famines of the world,
did that circumcised Priapus set you 
before my Veranius and Fabullus?
Do you attend sumptuously elegant banquets
daily, while companions of mine
seek invitations at the crossroads?
