LVI. ad Catonem

O rem ridiculam, Cato, et iocosam,
dignamque auribus et tuo cachinno!
ride quidquid amas, Cato, Catullum:
res est ridicula et nimis iocosa.
deprendi modo pupulum puellae
trusantem; hunc ego, si placet Dionae,
protelo rigida mea cecidi.

LVI. to Cato

O what a ridiculous business, Cato, and a funny one,
and worthy of your hearing and of your cackles!
Laugh however much you love Catullus, Cato:
the busines is silly and way too funny.
I caught in the way a boy thrusting hard
at a lass; this one I, if it please Venus,
jabbed with my stiffy, instead of a spear.
