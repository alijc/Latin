LVII. ad Gaium Iulium Caesarem

Pulcre convenit improbis cinaedis,
Mamurrae pathicoque Caesarique.
nec mirum: maculae pares utrisque,
urbana altera et illa Formiana,
impressae resident nec eluentur:
morbosi pariter, gemelli utrique,
uno in lecticulo erudituli ambo,
non hic quam ille magis vorax adulter,
rivales socii puellularum.
pulcre convenit improbis cinaedis.

LVII. to Gaius Julius Caesar

Apty it fits the wanton sodomites,
Mamurra and the boy toy Caesar.
And no wonder: their stains are alike,
one from the city and the other from Formia
they linger, stamped in, and will not wash off;
equally debauched, twins to each other,
both skilled in love in a single bed,
neither a more insatiable adulterer than the other,
the allied rivals of young girls.
