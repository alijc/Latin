LXXII. ad Lesbiam

Dicebas quondam solum te nosse Catullum,
     Lesbia, nec prae me velle tenere Iovem.
dilexi tum te non tantum ut vulgus amicam,
     sed pater ut gnatos diligit et generos.
nunc te cognovi: quare etsi impensius uror,
     multo mi tamen es vilior et levior.
qui potis est, inquis? quod amantem iniuria talis
     cogit amare magis, sed bene velle minus.

LXXII. to Lesbia

You used to say that you alone knew Catullus,
    Lesbia, and wouldn't want to hold Jupiter before me.
I loved you then, not so much as rabble >(girl)friend,
    but father as sons loves and sons-in-law.
Now >you I know: in what way even if more lavishly I am burned,
    by/to much to me nevertheless you are cheaper and more fickle.
Who is able, you say? That/because >lover/loving so great an offense 
    compels to love the more, but well to wish less.
