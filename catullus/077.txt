LXXVII. ad Rufum

Rufe mihi frustra ac nequiquam credite amice
     (frustra? immo magno cum pretio atque malo),
sicine subrepsti mi, atque intestina perurens
     ei misero eripuisti omnia nostra bona?
eripuisti, heu heu nostrae crudele venenum
     vitae, heu heu nostrae pestis amicitiae.

To Rufus

Rufus to me in vain and for nothing y'all believe, friend
   (in vain?  no indeed, with great price and evil),
thus you have crept up on me, and my guts, burning,
   to him to/by wretched you have snatched away all our good things?
You have snatched away, alas, alas, of our life cruelly poison,
   alas, alas the plague/destruction of our friendship.
