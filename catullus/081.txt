LXXXI. ad Iuventium

Nemone in tanto potuit populo esse, Iuventi,
     bellus homo, quem tu diligere inciperes.
praeterquam iste tuus moribunda ab sede Pisauri
     hospes inaurata palladior statua,
qui tibi nunc cordi est, quem tu praeponere nobis
     audes, et nescis quod facinus facias?

LXXXI. to Juventius

Can there really be in such a great populace, Juventius,
  no handsome man, whom you would undertake to love,
other than that man from the moribund seat of Pisaurum
  your host, the paler gilded statue,
who is now your heart, whom you dare to set before us,
  not knowing that you commit an outrage?
