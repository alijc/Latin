CVII. ad Lesbiam

Si quicquam cupido optantique optigit umquam
     insperanti, hoc est gratum animo proprie.
quare hoc est gratum nobis quoque carius auro
     quod te restituis, Lesbia, mi cupido.
restituis cupido atque insperanti, ipsa refers te
     nobis. o lucem candidiore nota!
quis me uno vivit felicior aut magis hac est
     optandus vita dicere quis poterit?

If anything ever happened to a longing and longed for and hopeless man,
  it is particularly pleasing to the soul.
Therefore it is also pleasing to us and dearer then gold
  that you, Lesbia, return to a longing me.
you return to a longing and hopeless man, you bear yourself back to us.
  O >light with a brighter sign!
that lives by me alone happier or more then is here
 longing life to say who can?
