CX. ad Aufilenam

Aufilena, bonae semper laudantur amicae:
     accipiunt pretium, quae facere instituunt.
tu, quod promisti, mihi quod mentita inimica es,
     quod nec das et fers saepe, facis facinus.
aut facere ingenuae est, aut non promisse pudicae,
     Aufillena, fuit: sed data corripere
fraudando officiis, plus quam meretricis avarae
     quae sese toto corpore prostituit.

To Aufillena

Aufillena, good girlfriends are always praised:
  they get a price, they who prepare to act.
You, because you promised, to me because you are deceived hostile,
  because and not you give and bring often, you do the deed.
or to do for a noble woman it is, or not to have promised to a chaste one,
  Aufillena, it was: but given to seize
to/by cheating to/by kindnesses, more than of a greedy harlot
  who herself prostitutes her whole body.
