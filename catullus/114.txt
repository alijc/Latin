CXIV. ad Mentulam

Firmanus saltu non falso Mentula diues
     fertur, qui tot res in se habet egregias,
aucupium omne genus, piscis, prata, arva ferasque.
     nequiquam: fructus sumptibus exsuperat.
quare concedo sit diues, dum omnia desint.
     saltum laudemus, dum modo ipse egeat.

to Mentula

firm/steady/loyal by leaping not falsely rich Mentula
is brought, who so many excellent things in himself has,
of the fowlers/spies each kind, fish, meadows, plowed fields and wild.
lest/not whoever: crops/fruit/profit to/by costs he/it surpasses.
how/why I allow that he might be rich, while all things are wanting.
