How The Mice Planned to Be Protected from the Cat

The mice undertook a plan in which they could protect themselves from the cat, and one of the wiser of them said to the others: "Let a bell be tied to the cat's neck. Then we'll be able to guard against it, and to hear it wherever it goes, and so avoid its ambush." This advice pleased everyone, and one said: "Who then among us is bold enough to tie the bell onto the cat's neck?" One mouse replied: "Certainly not me." Another replied: "I certainly don't dare to get near that cat, not for the whole world."

Moral. Generally, it happens in this way that the clergy, the monks and the subordinates rise up against their prelate, saying: "If only he could be removed and we would have a different prelate." That plan pleases everyone. In the end they say among themselves: "Who will set themselves up against him? Who will accuse him?" Then one and all, frightened, say: "Not me, surely not me"; and so the subordinates permit the prelates to survive.
