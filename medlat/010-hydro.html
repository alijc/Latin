The Water-Snake

A certain animal is called the water-snake, whose nature is to cover itself in clay, to be able to glide better. In the end it enters into a sleeping crocodile's mouth and, advancing into its stomach, bites its heart and so does away with the crocodile.

Moral. The water-snake signifies the son of God, who assumed the clay of our flesh in order to more easily be slipped into the mouth of the devil, and so, entering into his stomach and biting his heart, kills him.
