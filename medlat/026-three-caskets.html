The Three Caskets

Honorius, immensely rich, reigned, and had only one son, whom he loved dearly. The fame of this emperor flew throughout the world, because he was honest and just in all things. But he waged a war against a king and devastated him. After that king sustained many attacks and endless damage from the emperor, he finally thought: "I have only one daughter and my enemy only one son. If I could somehow join my daughter to his son then I would obtain everlasting peace." He sent distinguished messengers to the emperor to ask him to grant at least a temporary truce so that he might speak with him in person. The emperor took counsel and granted a one year's truce. Indeed the king came to him in person and offered his own daughter for the other's son. But the emperor said: "I will not do this unless I have two things. First, your daughter must be a virgin; Second, after your death your entire kingdom must be settled on my son." And the king said: "This pleases me well." A charter for the contract was sealed at once. The king bade the emperor farewell. Now when he had come to his own kingdom, he had a ship readied for the purpose of carrying his daughter across the sea to the emperor. When the ship was ready and every needed prepared, the maiden boarded with her rich dowery and five soldiers with ladies and maids. But while they were sailing across the sea a great whale rushed to meet them and tried to swallow the ship. When they saw this the sailors trembled with fear, and the maiden even more so. Indeed the sailors made a huge fire and stood guard day and night. But it fell out after three days that, exhausted from their vigil, they fell asleep. The whale suddenly swallowed the ship with all of its contents. The girl, when she understood that she was in the belly of a whale, cried out loudly. And the others were awoken by her cries. Indeed the sailors said to the maiden and the soldiers: "Dearest ones, take courage, God will save us; let us take good counsel, for we are in the belly of a whale." The maiden said: "Listen to my counsel and we will be saved." And they said: "Tell us." She said: "Let's light a large fire, and have someone injure the whale as deeply as possible and by these two things it will receive its death-blow and will at once float to land, and so by God's grace we will escape." Indeed they fulfilled the maiden's advice in every way. The whale sensed its death and headed for land. Hard by that beach there was a certain soldier stationed, who was taking an after-dinner amble along the seashore. So when he saw the whale floating hither and thither and approaching the land, he calls his servants and they dragged the whale to land. And they began to strike it with tools. The maiden, when she heard the noise spoke before them all and said: "Dearest, strike gently and open the flank of the whale; we here in its belly are the sons of good men of noble birth." The soldier, when he heard the voice of the maiden said to his servants: "Dearest, open the flank of the whale and let us see what might lie hidden within." Indeed when it was opened, the maiden came out first, more dead than alive, and then the soldiers and everyone else. She began to relate whose daughter she was, and that she was destined to be the wife of the emperor's son. On hearing this, the soldier kept her and her retinue with him for several days, until they had recovered their health. After that he sent the maiden with her gifts and her whole retinue to the emperor. The emperor, when he saw her, said: "Dearest daughter, may it go well with you now and forever. But I tell you daughter, before you have my son in holy matrimony, I will test you with a single deed." He immediately had three caskets made. The first was of pure gold and costly stones. And there was such an inscription upon the casket: "He who opens me, will find in me that which he deserves." And the whole casket was filled with the bones of the dead. The second was of pure silver, studded with gems from every part of the world, and which had this inscription: "He who chooses me, will find in me that which nature gives." This casket was filled with dirt. The third casket was of lead, having this inscription: "I choose to be here and to rest, rather than to remain in the king's treasury." In this casket were three precious rings. Then the emperor said to the maiden: "Dearest, here are three caskets; choose whichever one you will; and if you choose well, you will get my son in marriage." Indeed she closely considered the three caskets and said in her heart: "May God, who sees all, give me such grace to choose so that I might not fail of that for which I have worked so hard." And she touched the first casket and read the writing: "He who," etc. She thought: "The casket is outwardly precious, but I know not what might be hidden within it, likewise I am unwilling to choose it." Next she read the second. And she said: "Never did nature give that my father's daughter ought to be joined to the emperor's son." And likewise, etc. The third casket she read, saying: "It is better to me to rest with the son of a king than in my father's treasury." And in a loud voice she cried: "I choose this third casket." The emperor, when he heard, said: "O noble maiden, you have chosen wisely enough. In this casket are my three precious rings; one for me, one for my son, and the third for you in a sign of betrothal." He had the nuptials celebrated at once, and delivered his son to her, and so they finished their lives in peace.

The moral. Dearly beloved, the emperor is God who has long waged war with mankind, in so much as the entire human nature was destroyed through sin. In this way the truce was sent to us through the Lord, that is, Christ. The daughter, who was destined to be betrothed to the son of the emperor, is the soul. Therefore it is right for the ship to be prepared for her with messengers, that is, the body in which the soul resides with the five senses and so forth, the sailors are reason, will, etc. But it is fit to cross the sea, that is, the world. The great whale is the devil, against whom we need to be vigilant. But if it befalls us to fall asleep in our sins then he wil swallow us, body and soul. And so, do as the maiden did; light the fire of devotion and strike with tools, that is, good works, until he withdraws and lets slip his power over you. Then serve the soldiers, that is, the preachers and confessors, who have to strike him, until the maiden, that is, the soul, leaves his power and comes to the court of God. But we must understand what the three caskets represent to her. From the first casket we can understand the power and riches that have such an inscription, that is, when the body and soul are divided, God finds nothing in me but the sins that I have earned, at which we must suffer; woe to those who choose this one. From the second it is understood the sages of the world, whose speeches glitter like silver and on the inside are filled with earthly matters, with such an inscription: "He who chooses me," etc. Nature always seeks after the soul's opposite and those are not wed to Christ. From the third casket are choosen good christians, who are made of lead, that is, that they do not worry about any worldly thing; in which are three rings, of course, faith, hope and charity; he who chooses these, can have the son of God more gladly than to remain in the treasury of the world. Let us so strive.
