A Certain Hermit

There once was a hermit who had two cloaks, one for feast days and the other for everyday. There came one winter a poor man, seeking clothing. However, he held out a loaf of bread and gave it to him, saying. "Take this, for I have no extra clothing to give you." But the poor man kept on weeping and wailing, saying: "Have pity on me." And so the monk, overcome by the poor man's entreaty, went into his cell and pulled off his everyday cloak and gave it to the poor man; but he keep the better one for himself. But later, stricken with remorse, he said to the poor man: "Give me back the cloak that I gave you and take this better one."

Now after the poor man had taken it, he put it on and, going out into the city, sold it to a certain prostitue. And she put it on and came once and again to where that monk was. Now when he saw his own clothing, he recognized it as the one that he used to wear on feast days. He was most grieved and, going into his cell, said: "Truly, what I did did not please God, and he has not accepted my devotion, seeing that my cloak, which I used to wear on feast days, has come into such hands as these, and a mere prostitue wears it."

Indeed, on account of his sorrow he did not eat that day. But that night, when he fell asleep he saw our Lord Jesus Christ, clothed in the garment that he had given the poor man, who had entered his cell and said to him: "Brother, brother." And as he looked, he saw a great light and said: "Who are you, Lord?" And he said to him: "Look at me." But as he stared, he said to him: "Do you recognize this?" He answered: "Even so, Lord: it is my cloak." He said to him: "I am Jesus. Sorrow no more, but rejoice and be of good cheer and give thanks to God. For what you gave to the poor man, I have received." And the monk took great joy in the kindness of our Lord Jesus Christ, who did not allow him to be tormented by his thoughts, but gave him assurance in his word what he had said before, that "what you have done for the least of mine, you have done for me."
