As Was Said Before

This same Abbot Palladius also related to us what a certain lover of Christ had done when he was living in Alexandria. He was intensely religious and a giver of alms and a protector of the poor and of monks, who had a wife who was faithful and who feared God and fasted every day; indeed they had a single daughter, six years of age. But one day this man wished to sail to Constantinople; for he was a trader. And he left his wife and son in the house with a single servant. Indeed, when he departed from his house and went to the ship, his wife said to him with tears in her eyes: "To whom are you abandoning us, my lord?" And he replied, saying: "To your holy mistress, the mother of God and virgin, Mary."

One day, while the woman was sitting with her daughter to do their work, the servant, inflamed by the devil, wished to kill the mother and her daughter and to steal whatever they had in the house and flee. Indeed, he took a sword and climbed up from the kitchen into the diningroom, where the mother and daughter were. And when he had drawn near to the door to the dining room, this most wicked servant was struck blind, and was helpless to either climb up to the diningroom or to go down to the kitchen. But when, after an hour, he had gathered up his strength to enter and could not, he began to call his mistress to come to him. But when she fell to astonishment about how he was standing in the middle of the doorway and did not prceed to her. And she told him: "You come to me; for that's what is proper." For she didn't realize that he had become blind. Indeed the servant began the more to call her and to swear with her to go and draw near to him. But she swore that she would by no means go to him. And he said: "At least send the girl to me." And the woman replied to him: "Neither the girl nor I will come to you. I already told you, if you want to come, then come; but if not, then stay there." Then he saw that he availed nothing of the things that he had intended to do, and snatched the sword and stabbed himself in the guts. But his mistress, when she saw that the wicked servant had done himself in, cried out. When the neighbors heard this they came running, together with the guards and found him half-dead and barely breathing. And when they asked him why he had done this thing, he confessed before them all what he had wanted to do; and then expired. Then everyone praised God, who had saved the mother and daughter and who saves everyong through the aid of his holy mother. Amen.
