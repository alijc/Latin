XXXIV

DE FAMILIARITATE MULIERUM PERICULOSA

On Perilous Familiarity with Women

Narravit mihi quidam homo valde religiosus quod in partibus in quibus commoratus fuerat accidit quod quaedam valde religiosa et honesta matrona, frequenter veniens ad ecclesiam die ac nocte, devotissime Domino serviebat.

A certain intensely religious man told me that in the region in which he used to live it happened that a certain devout and honest matron used to serve the Lord with the greatest devotion, coming often to the church, day and night.

Quidam autem monachus, custos et thesaurarius monasterii, magnum nomen religionis habebat et revera ita erat.

Now a certain monk, the custodian and treasurer of the monastery, had a great name for piety and was in fact pious.

Cum autem frequenter in ecclesia de iis quae pertinent ad religionem mutuo loquerentur, diabolus invidens honestati et famae eorum immisit eis vehementes temptationes, ita quod amor spiritualis conversus est in carnalem, unde dixerunt sibi et assignaverunt noctem in qua recederet monachus a monasterio cum thesauro ecclesiae, et matrona a domo sua cum summa pecuniae quam auferret clam marito.

But as they often talked between themselves in the church about those things that concern religion, the devil, envious of their honesty and reputation, sent powerful temptations upon them, so that their spiritial love was converted into carnal lust. From this they spoke to each other and assigned a night for the monk to slip out of the monastery with the church treasury, and the matron from her house with as much money as she could carry off without her husband knowing.

Cum autem sic discederent et fugerent, monachi surgentes ad matutinas viderunt arcas fractas et thesaurum ecclesiae asportatum, et cum non invenirent monachum festinanter secuti sunt eum.

Now after they so withdrew and fled, the monks, rising for matins, saw that the chests had been broken and the church treasury carried off, and since they couldn't find the monk they quickly followed him.

Similiter maritus dictae mulieris, videns arcam suam apertam et pecuniam ablatam, secutus est uxorem suam et, apprehendentes monachum et mulierem cum thesauro, reduxerunt eos et in arto carcere posuerunt.

Similarly, the husband of the aforesaid woman, seeing that his chest was open and the money removed, chased after his wife and, catching the monk and the woman with the treasury, brought them back and put them into a strong jail.

AJC Then the monk came to himself, and began to pray, with many tears, to the blessed Virgin, whom he had always served from infancy and had never had anything like this happen to him.

AJC And likewise the aforesaid matron began to urgently beg for help from the Blessed Virgin, whom she had been often wont to greet, and to genuflect before her statue.

AJC Finally the intensely angry Blessed Virgin appeared to them and, after she'd strongly reproached them, said: "Would that I could obtain forgiveness of your sin from my Son: but what can I do about such a scandal?"

AJC For you have made a stench for religious people before the whole district, so that henceforth they won't be trusted.

AJC Indeed, this damage is like to be irreparable.

AJC But in the end the pious Virgin, overcome by their prayers, forced the demons that had managed this affair to appear, charging them that, since they had defamed religion, therefore they should put an end to the infamy.
