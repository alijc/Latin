On Perilous Familiarity with Women

A certain intensely religious man told me that in the region where he used to live it happened that a certain devout and honest matron served the Lord with the greatest devotion, coming to church often, day and night. Now a certain monk, the custodian and treasurer of the monastery, had a great name for piety and was in fact pious. But as they often talked between themselves in the church about those things that concern religion, the devil, envious of their honesty and reputation, sent powerful temptations upon them, so that their spiritial love was converted into carnal lust. From this they spoke to each other and assigned a night for the monk to slip out of the monastery with the church treasury, and the matron from her house with as much money as she could carry off without her husband knowing. Now after they so withdrew and fled, the monks, rising for matins, saw that the chests had been broken and the church treasury carried off, and since they couldn't find the monk they quickly followed him. Similarly, the husband of the aforesaid woman, seeing that his chest was open and the money removed, chased after his wife and, catching the monk and the woman with the treasury, brought them back and put them into a strong jail. Now there was such a great scandal through the whole district and everyone dishonored religious people, so that there was a greater injury from the disgrace and scandal than from the sin itself. Then the monk came to his senses, and began to pray, with many tears, to the blessed Virgin, whom he had always served from infancy and had never had anything like this happen to him. And likewise the aforesaid matron began to urgently beg for help from the Blessed Virgin (to whom she had been wont to pay her respects, day and night) and to genuflect before her statue. Finally the intensely angry Blessed Virgin appeared to them and, after she'd strongly reproached them, said: "I could obtain forgiveness of your sin from my Son: but what can I do about such a scandal?" For you have made a stench for religious people before the whole district, so that henceforth they won't be trusted. Indeed, this damage is like to be irreparable. But in the end the pious Virgin, overcome by their prayers, compelled the demons that had managed this affair to appear, charging them that, since they had defamed religion, they should therefore put an end to the infamy. And indeed, since they couldn't withstand her commands, they worked out (after great care and many changes of plan) a way to be free of the disgrace. They restored the monk to his church in the night, restoring the broken chest to its previous state, and putting the treasure back into it. Likewise, they closed and locked the chest that the matron had opened and put the money back into it, and they put the woman in her room in the spot where she was wont to pray. Now when the monks discovered that their order's treasure was back in its usual place they worshiped the Lord. And the husband found his wife, and they found their treasure just as it had been before. They were struck dumb and commenced to marvel, and, running to the jail they saw the monk and the woman in shackles, just as they'd left them earlier. In fact, what seemed so to them was that one demon had transformed himself into the likeness of the monk and another into the likeness of the woman. Now when the whole town had come together to see the miracle, the demons, in the hearing of everyone, said: "We withdraw; for it is enough that we mocked them and made religious people think evil thoughts." And, having said this, they vanished in the blink of an eye. Then everyone begged pardon at the feet of the abused monk and woman. See how much disgrace and scandal and undeserving injury would have been managed by the devil against religious people, had not the blessed Virgin aided them.
