Resistance to the Law of God is Futile

AJC In the time of this Conrad, which is, of course, 1025 AD, Count Leopold, as is asserted in a chronicle, fearing the king's anger, fled with his wife to lie low in a cottage on an island.

AJC Once, when the Emperor was hunting in that wood with night coming on, it behooved them to offer him hospitality in that cottage.

AJC Now the lady of the house was with child, and close to giving birth, she properly, while she was able, made up beds and attended to their needs.

AJC That night the woman bore a son and, in the third hour, the Emperor heard a voice coming to him: "Conrad, this new-born boy will be your son-in-law."

AJC In the morning he got up and, calling two attendants and confidential secretaries to him, said: "Go and tear this little boy out of his mother's arms by force, cut him in half and bring his heart to me."

AJC They ran off headlong and snatched the boy from his mother's lap.

AJC But when they looked at his most attractive form they were moved to pity, and put him in a tree to be devoured by wild beasts, and, after cutting a hare in half, took its heart to the Emperor.

AJC On that very day a certain duke was crossing through that place and heard the boy squalling and had him fetched and, as he had no son of his own, took him to his wife and got him a wet-nurse. He put out the story that his wife had given birth to him, and called him Henry.

AJC And so, after he had grown up, he was gorgeous to look at, well-spoken, and agreeble to everyone.
