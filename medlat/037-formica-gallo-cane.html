AJC On the Ant, the Cock and the Dog

AJC Balaam, who is called Lucaman in Arabic, said to his son: Son, let not the ant be wiser than you, that it gathers in the summer the wherewithal to live in the winter.

AJC Son, let not the cock be more watchful than you, that it wakes in the morning, and you sleep.

AJC Son, let not the cock be firmer than you, that it keeps order among its ten wives, while you cannot correct your single one.

AJC Son, let not the dog be nobler in heart than you, that it does not forget its benefactors, but you do forget your benefactors.

AJC Son, let it not seem to you that having one enemy is a small thing, or having a thousand friends is a great one.

AJC I say to you: Example I. About half a friend.

AJC An Arab who was about to die summoned his son and said: "Tell me, my son, how many friends did you gain during my lifetime."

AJC And his son replied, saying: "I gained, I think, a hundred friends."

AJC His father said: "A philosopher said: 'You should not praise a friend until you have tested him.'

AJC Indeed, I was born before you and have gained scarcely half a friend.

AJC How then have you gained a hundred?

AJC Therefore go and test them all, so that you might learn if any of them is fully your friend."

AJC The son said: "How do you advise me to test them?"
