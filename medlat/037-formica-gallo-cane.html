On the Ant, the Cock and the Dog

AJC Balaam, who caled Lucaman in Arabic, said to his son: Son, is not the ant wiser than you, for it gathers in the summer the wherewithall to live in the winter.

AJC Son, is not the cock more watchful than you, for it wakes in the morning, and you sleep.

AJC Son, is not the cock firmer than you, for it keeps order among its ten wives, while you cannot correct your single one.

AJC Son, is not the dog nobler in heart than you, for it does not forget its benefactors, but you forget your benefactors.

AJC Son, does it not seem to you that having one enemy is a small thing, or having a thousand friends is a great one.

AJC I say to you: Example I. About half a friend.

AJC An Arab who was about to die called his son and said: "Tell me, my son, how many friends did you gain while I lived."

AJC And his son replied, saying: "I gained, I think, a hundred friends."

AJC His father said: "A philosopher said: 'You should not praise a friend until you test him.'
