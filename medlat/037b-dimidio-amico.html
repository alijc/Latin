I say to you: Example I. About half a friend.

An Arab who was about to die summoned his son and said: "Tell me, my son, how many friends did you gain during my lifetime." And his son replied, saying: "I gained, I think, a hundred friends." His father said: "A philosopher said: 'You should not praise a friend until you have tested him.' Indeed, I was born before you and have gained scarcely half a friend for myself. How then have you gained a hundred? Therefore go and test them all, so that you might learn whether any of them is fully your friend." The son said: "How do you advise me to test them?" The father said: "Put a calf that has been killed and cut into bits into a sack, so that the blood seeps through to the outside of the sack. And when you come to a friend, tell him: 'Buddy, I have accidentally killed a man; I beg you to bury him in secret; for no one will suspect you, and so you can save me.'" The son did as his father commanded.
