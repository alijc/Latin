Example II. On the Perfect Friend

And the father said: Example II. On the perfect friend.

A story was told to me about two merchants, one of whom was in Egypt, and the other in Babylon, and they knew each other by hearsay alone, and would send for what they needed through intermediaries. Now it happened that the Babylonian went to Egypt for business. When the Egyptian heard of his arrival he hurried to meet him and received him, rejoicing, into his home and served him in everything for eight days, as is the custom among friends, and showed him all sorts of poetry that he had in his house.

START

AJC At the end of eight days he fell ill.

AJC Because of the deep feeling that the host bore towards his friend, he brought in all the Egyptian doctors to examine his guest-friend.

AJC Indeed the doctors, after taking his pulse and inspecting his urine again and again, didn't recognize his illness.

AJC And because they discerned no physical illness through this, they understood it to be the suffering of love.
