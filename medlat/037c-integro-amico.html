Example II. On the Perfect Friend

AJC And the father said: Example II. On the perfect friend.

AJC Something was related to me about two traders, one of whom was in Egypt, and the other in Babylon, and they knew each other by hearsay alone and would send through intermediaries by necessity for themselves.

AJC Now it happened that the Babylonian went to Egypt for business.

AJC When the Egyptian heard of his arrival he hurried to meet him and received him, rejoicing, into his home and served him in everything for eight days, as is the custom among friends, and showed him all sorts of poetry that he had in his house.

AJC At the end of eight days he fell ill.
