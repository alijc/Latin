The Servant Maimundus

A youth asked an old man: “When I am invited to dine, what should I do? Should I eat only a little food, or a great deal of it?”

And the old man said to him: "A great deal. Because if it was your friend who invited you, he'll be very glad; but it was an enemy, he'll be sorry."

When the boy heard this he laughed. The old man said to him: "Why do you laugh?"

Boy: “I was reminded of what I heard about black Maimundus. For a certain old man asked him how much he could eat. And he said to him: 'From whose meal, mine or someone else's?'

And he said: 'From yours.'

Maimundus: 'As little as I can.'

The old man: 'How much from someone else's?'

Maimundus: 'As much as I can.' ”

Old man: “You were just reminded of the story of a certain gluttonous, lazy, stupid, chattering and worthless fool, and whatever is said of such like about him, even more is invented about him.”

Youth: “It pleases me a great deal to hear about him, because everything about him is laughable. And if you can call to mind anything about his sayings or deeds, please tell me, and I will take it as a gift.”

Old man: “One night his master ordered him to bar the door. In fact he, overwhelmed with laziness, could not bring himself to rise and therefore said that the door had been barred. Now when morning came, the master told his servant: 'Maimundus, open the door.' And the servant told him: 'Master, I knew that you would want it to be opened today and therefore I didn't want to bar it at a late hour.' That was when the master first realized that he had given up on account of his laziness. He said: 'Get up and do your work, because it is day and the sun is already up.' The servant said: 'Master, if the sun is up then give me food.' And his master said: 'Worthless servant, do you want to eat tonight?' Servant: 'If it's night then let me sleep.'

On another night the master said to his servant: 'Maimundus, get up and see whether or not it is raining.' But in fact, he called the dog, that was lying outside the door, and after it came in he felt the dog's feet. And, after finding that they were dry, said to his master: 'Master, it isn't raining'.

On another night the master asked his servant whether there was a fire in the house. In fact he called the cat and felt whether or not it was warm. And when he found it cold, said: 'No, master.'”

The youth: “I heard about his laziness; but I wish to hear about his talkativeness.”
