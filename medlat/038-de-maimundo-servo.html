The Servant Maimundus

A youth asked an old man: “When I am invited to dine, what should I do? Should I eat only a little food, or a great deal of it?”

And the old man said to him: "A great deal. Because if it was your friend who invited you, he'll be very glad; but it was an enemy, he'll be sorry."

When the boy heard this he laughed. The old man said to him: "Why do you laugh?"

Boy: “I was reminded of what I heard about black Maimundus. For a certain old man asked him how much he could eat. And he said to him: 'From whose meal, mine or someone else's?'

And he said: 'From yours.'

Maimundus: 'As little as I can.'

The old man: 'How much from someone else's?'

Maimundus: 'As much as I can.' ”

Old man: “You were just reminded of the story of a certain gluttonous, lazy, stupid, chattering and worthless fool, and whatever is said of such like about him, even more is invented about him.”

Youth: “It pleases me a great deal to hear about him, because everything about him is laughable. And if you can call to mind anything about his sayings or deeds, please tell me, and I will take it as a gift.”
