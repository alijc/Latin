Two City Slickers and a Yokel

An Arab admonished his son: "Son, if you are on the road with a companion, value him as much as yourself and make no plans to deceive him, lest you are likewise deceived, as happened to two city slickers and a country yokel."

The son said, "Father, tell me, so that our descendents might get some use from the tale."

The father said, “This was told about two city slickers and a country yokel, on the occasion of a visit to Mecca, because they were messmates until they got nearly to Mecca, and then they ran short of food so that they barely had enough flour to make one small loaf of bread.

The city slickers, when they saw this, said to each other: 'We don't have enough bread and our companion eats too much. Therefore, let us make a plan on how we can carry off part of the bread for ourselves, as is our due, so that we alone could eat it.'

Then they made a plan of this sort, that they would make a loaf of bread and bake it and sleep while it was baking and whichever of them saw marvels in his dreams, he alone would eat the loaf. They said this craftily, for they regarded the yokel as gullible to this sort of trick. And they made a loaf and put it in the fire, then lay down to sleep. But the yokel saw through their trick and pulled the half-baked bread out of the fire while his companions were sleeping and ate it and lay back down.

But one of the city slickers, as if he was frightened in his sleep, woke up and called his companion. And the other city slicker said to him: 'What's wrong?'

But he said: 'I've had a marvelous dream; for it seemed to me that two angels opened the gates of heaven and picked me up and led me before God.'

And his companion said to him: 'It is a marvel, this dream that you had. But I dreamt that I, with two angels leading me and splitting earth open, was led into hell.'

In truth, the yokel heard all this and yet feigned sleep. But the city slickers, cheated and willing to cheat, called the yokel to wake him.

Indeed, the yokel replied, cleverly and as if he were frightened: 'Who is it that's calling me?'

But they said: 'We are your companions.'

And the yokel said: 'Are you back already?'

But they countered: 'Where did we go that we needed to come back from there?'

The yokel said to them: 'Now it seemed to me that two angels took one of you and opened the gates of heaven and led him before God; then two other angels took the other and, having opened the earth, led him into hell. And, having seen this, I thought that neither of you would be ever coming back and got up and ate the bread.'“

And the father said: "Son, it happens thus to those who wish to cheat their companion, because they will be cheated by their own nature."

Then the son said: "So it happened to them, as has been said in the proverb: 'He who wants it all, loses it all.'
