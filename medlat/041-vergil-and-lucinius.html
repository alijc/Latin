Vergil and Lucinius

AJC There flourished during the same time of Rome that most renowned poet, Virgil, who, having been born in from Mantua, a Sicilian city, was quite well known to the king, for he had even been honored by him with gifts.

AJC Therefore, on account of his fame and because he at that time spent his time among philosophers in particular, a father sent his son to him with great gifts, entreating him by his gods that he use his knowledge both to provide for the boy and to most carefully protect him from spiteful treachery.

AJC Timebat enim pater ne aliquid sinistri per invidiam pateretur, quia hoc a divinis et astrorum peritis acceperat. --> For the father feared lest the son suffer some sinister thing through hatred, for he had learned this from prophets and from those expert in astrology.

AJC Now Virgil, taking the boy in because of the respect and friendship of the king, in fact first instructed him in the rudiments of the letters and then, coaxing and cajoling, as is the custom of teachers, he throroughly taught him, within a narrow space of time, to construct a syllable from letters, and to form a sentence from syllables, and from sentences to actually complete an oration.
