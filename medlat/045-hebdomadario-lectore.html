On the Weekly Reader

AJC When the brethren are eating at table a reading ought not to be lacking.

AJC Let no-one pick a book to read there by chance, but let the one who is to read proceed through the whole Lord's week.

AJC Let him, commencing after the masses and communion, ask everyone to pray that God take the spirit of pride away from him; and let him speak this line three times with everyone, but starting himself: "Lord, open my lips and my mouth will announce your praise"; and so when the benediction has been recieved, let him procede to the reading.

AJC And let there be complete silence at the table, so that nobody's whisper or voice might be heard there, except for the voice of the the one who is reading.

AJC Let the brothers take turns to serve each other what is really needed for eating and drinking so that no-one needs to ask for anything.

AJC But if there should be any need, it's better to ask for it by some sort of noise and sign than by voice; and let no-one ask anything about the reading or anything else, or be given the opportunity; except the prior might perhaps wish to briefly say something in explanation.

AJC But let the brother reader for the week have some bread and wine before he begins to read, for the sake of holy communion, and to sustain him from serious misfortune in his fast; but afterwards let him restore himself with the cooks and servers of the week.

AJC But not all the brothers in the order shoule read or sing, but only those who can edify their listeners.
