AJC On Daily Manual Labor

AJC Idleness is harmful for the soul and therefore during set times the brothers should be occupied in manual labor, and also in set times in sacred readings.

AJC Therefore, by this arrangement we trust by which time to be arranged: it is so that from Easter to the first of October passing from the hand from the first hour to the hour nearly fourth they should produce what will be needed; but from the fourth hour to the hour in which they celebrate sext they should be free from reading.

AJC But after rising from the table after sext they should rest in their beds in total silence, or perchance one who wishes to read may thus read to himself so that he doesn't disturb another.

AJC It should be done a bit before the ninth hour, halfway into the eighth hour, and then what needs doing should be done until vespers.

AJC But if need of place or poverty expels so that to gathering the crops through self they might be occupied, they should not be discouraged because then truly/in spring are monks if by/to work of their hands they live like both our fathers and the apostles.

AJC Nevertheless measure all things they might become on account of the fainthearted.
