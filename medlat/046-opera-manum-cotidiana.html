AJC On Daily Manual Labor

AJC Idleness is harmful for the soul, and therefore the brothers should be occupied in manual labor at set times, and in sacred readings at set times in turn.

AJC Therefore, by this arrangement we trust whether to be regulated by time: that is, for them to go outside the first thing in the morning from Easter to the first of October and produce what's needed from the first hour to almost the fourth. But from the fourth hour to the hour in which they celebrate sext they should be free from reading.

AJC But after rising from the table after sext they should rest in their beds in total silence, or perchance one who wishes to read may thus read to himself so that he doesn't disturb anyone else.

AJC This should be done a bit before the ninth hour, halfway into the eighth hour, and then what needs doing should be done until vespers.

AJC But if the needs or the difficult straits of the place require that they themselves be employed in gathering crops in, then they should not be discouraged, because they are then truly monks, if they live by work of their own hands, like both our fathers and the apostles.

AJC Nevertheless all things should be in due measure, for the sake of the fainthearted.
