AJC XLVII

Attila in Italy

AJC In fact, Attila, had got an opportunity from the Visigoths' retreat and perceived a long hoped-for division among the enemy throughout the region. He was soon free from danger and shifted his army to overthrow the Romans. And with the first assault he besieged the city of Aquileia, the chief city of Venice, positioned on a sharp point or tongue of an Adriatic bay, whose walls are lapped by the Natissa River, flowing from the east from Mount Piccis.
