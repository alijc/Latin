XLVII Attila in Italy

In fact, Attila, had got an opportunity from the Visigoths' retreat and perceived a long hoped-for division among the enemy throughout the region. He was soon free from danger and shifted his army to overthrow the Romans. And with the first assault he besieged the city of Aquileia, the chief city of Venice, positioned on a sharp point or tongue of an Adriatic bay, whose walls are lapped by the Natissa River, flowing from the east from Mount Piccis.

AJC But after the siege had been under way for many days with no breaking through their defenses, being resisted from the inside by very powerful Romans, and with his army now murmuring and desiring to withdraw, Attila is strolling around the walls, while he ponders whether to break camp or yet delay. He notices white birds, that is, storks, that nest on the gables of the houses, to have their young around the city, as opposed the custom through the country to bear them outside.
