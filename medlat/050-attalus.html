AJC THE STORY OF ATTALUS

AJC Now Theudericus and Childebertus entered into a treaty and, after pledging each other an oath that neither would move against the other, accepted hostages from each another, by which means what had been declared would be more easily confirmed.

AJC Then many senators' sons were given into this captivity: but after a quarrel rose up again between the kings they were sentenced to common servitude, and anyone could take them into custody.

AJC While many of them slipped away in flight and returned to their native land. some were held fast in servitude.

AJC Among these was Attalus, the nephew of the blessed Gregory, bishop of Langres. He was given over into common servitude and sent to the horse stables.

AJC For he was working in the territory of Treves for a foreign person.

AJC Finally, the blessed Gregory arranged for servants to investigate. And when they found Attalus, they offered gifts to his master.

AJC But he rejected them, saying: "One of such birth needs ten pounds of gold to be redeemed."

AJC Upon their return, a certain Leo from their lord's kitchen said: "If you would let me go, perhaps I could fetch him back from captivity."

AJC Now the lord was happy to do it, so straightway he went to the spot and wanted to steal the boy away, but could not.

AJC Then, bargaining with a certain man, he said: "Come with me and sell me in the house of that foreigner, and let my price be a profit to you, while I'll have freer access to do what I have resolved."
