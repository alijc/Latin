On the Gardener Monk in the Same Monastery

Gregory. Felix, who is called Bent, whom you yourself know well, was recently placed in command of his monastery. He used to tell me many wonders about the brothers of his monastery; I hold back a few of these that come to mind, because I hasten to others.

AJC But I'll tell one of his stories that I don't think should be at all neglected.

AJC In his monastery, a certain monk of great life was the gardener.

AJC Indeed, a thief used to come and climb over the fence and secretly steal vegetables.

AJC And while he was transplanting there were many that he couldn't find, and he noticed some trampled underfoot, with others uprooted. Going round the whole garden, he found the path where the thief would come.

AJC And as he was strolling around his garden, he also discovered a serpent, and ordering it, he said: "Follow me." And after reaching the place where the thief got in, he commanded the serpent, saying: "In the name of Jesus I order you to guard this entrance and to keep the thief from getting through here."

AJC The snake immediately stretched out across the path, and the monk returned to his cell.
