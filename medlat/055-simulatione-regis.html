On the Revelation of King Totila's Deceit

AJC In the times of the Goths, after their king Totila heard that a holy man had a prophetic soul, he went to his monastery and halted a little way away and sent word that he had come to visit him.

AJC He commanded the man to come to him from the monastery immediately, but in the meantime, since he was of a deceitful bent, he endeavored to test whether a man of God could have a prophetic soul.

AJC Now a certain man, called Riggo, was his swordbearer. The king gave him his own shoes and made him get dressed in royal garments, and instructed him to proceed to the man of God as if in his own person.

AJC And he sent three counts in his retinue, namely Vulteric, Ruderic and Blidin, who were accustomed to stick close to him in front of everyone else, to walk beside Riggo before the eyes of the servant of God, pretending that he was King Totila. And he also gave him attendants and swordbearers so that he'd be taken as the king, as much from these same attendants as from his purple garments.
