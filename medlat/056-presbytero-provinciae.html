On a Priest of the Province of Valeria, Who Held a Thief at his Grave

Now it was in the province of Valeria that the matter that I'm relating happened, and was made known to me by the account of my abbot Valentine of blessed memory.

Indeed, there was a certain venerable priest there, who, along with his clerks, earnest in the glories of God and good works, led a life of pious conduct. Now when the day of his death came, he passed away and was buried in front of the church. Indeed, there was a sheepfold next to that same church, and if they strained, those sheep could get into where he was buried.

Now one night, when the clerics were inside the church singing psalms, a thief came to enter the sheepfold to commit a theft. He stole a wether and quickly left. And when he got to the place where the man of God was buried, he suddenly got stuck fast and couldn't move a step. Indeed, he let the wether drop from his neck and wanted to release it, but was not able to open his hand.
