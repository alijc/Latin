About the Fable

AJC Poets have called fables, from what is to be spoken, because they are not matters of fact, but only fictions to be spoken.

AJC For that reason these are introduced so that, by means of fictional dumb animals speaking among themselves, the likeness of a certain mode of life of men might be recognized.

AJC It is related that Alcmaeon of Croton first invented these, and they are called the Aesopica, for he defiled at Phrygia in this matter.

AJC But the fables are either the Aesopica or the Lybistica.

AJC The Aesopica are when dumb animals are contrived to converse among themselves, or things that don't have an soul, like cities, trees, mountains, rocks, or rivers.

AJC But the Lybistica are when the spoken fellowship of men with beasts or of beasts with men is contrived.

AJC Poets adapted some fables for amusement, and others to the nature of things, while a few were adapted to the character of men.

AJC Plautus and Terentius composed these sort of fictions, as they generally called them, for amusement's sake.

AJC They adapted fables to the nature of things, like "Lame Vulcan", since fire was never ruled by means of nature; like that threefold beast, "a lion in front, a snake behind, and the very middle a chimera"; that is, a she-goat, the ages of man purposefully distinguishing themselves through it; of which youth is the wild and rough first part, like the lion; middle age is very bright, like the goat, because it sees so clearly; then comes old age with its bending calamities, like the snake.
