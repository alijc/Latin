On Days and on Night

AJC On days.

AJC Day is the presence of the sun, or when the sun is above the earth, just as night is when the sun is below the earth.

AJC For in order for it to be day or night, the cause is that the sun is either above the earth, or below the earth.

AJC A proper day is made up of twenty-four hours, the time it takes for a day and a night to complete the circuit of its course in a circular motion from the east until the next eastern sun in the sky.

AJC Now it's wrong to call the sun's circuit from east to west a single day.

AJC But there are two intervals of the day, diurnal and nocturnal; and a day really consists of twenty-four hours, but an interval of twelve hours.

AJC But it's called a day from its more useful part.

AJC From this too, it's common to speak of a number of days without mentioning nights, just as it was also written in the divine law: "There was made in the evening and the morning one day."

AJC According to the Egyptians the day begins at sundown; according to the Persians, at sunrise; according to the Athenians, at the sixth hour of the day; according to the Romans, in the middle of the night.

AJC From that time also comes the crow of the cock, whose voice proclaims the day, when the midnight breeze might also be.
