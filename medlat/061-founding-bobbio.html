THE FOUNDING OF BOBBIO

Therefore, as we have said before, after Saint Columbanus saw that Theudebertus was defeated by Theudeberis, he left Gaul and Germany and came to Italy, where he was honorably received by Agilulfus, the king of the Lombards. And when he was given the choice of living anywhere in Italy that he wished, there by God's will it came to pass, while he was staying at the city of Milan and was determined that the crimes of heresy, that is, Arian falsehoods, be burnt, torn up and cut out of the scriptures (he even published a book, with flowery language, against these), a certain man, Iocundus (Pleasant) by name, came to the king, and declared to him that he had knowledge of a church of Saint Peter, the first of the apostles, in the wilderness in the Appennine countryside, in which he might put his virtues to the test, places fruitful with abundance, running waters, plenty of fish.

And by tradition they called this ancient place Bobbio, from the name of the stream that flows in that place and another river flowing forth, called Trivea; upon which Hannibal once wintered, and experienced most terrible losses, of men, of horses, and of elephants.

And after he got there, he returned, with every intention of obtaining the half-ruined church in that place and restoring it to its former glory. And in this restoration the wonderful power of the Lord was revealed.

AJC For when through steep brooms of rocks fir trees were chopped down in the middle of thick, inaccessible woodlands, or cut down elsewhere, and by chance felled into rough places that wagons couldn't approach, in a wondrous manner the trunk, which thirty or forty men could barely suceed in carrying on level ground, there with just two or three, on a course afflicted with steep, rocky paths, the man of God, climbing up, laid a heavy weight on his shoulders and on those of his men; and where before this, in view of the roughness of the road, they could barely walk with unconstrained steps, now, burdened with the weight of the trunks they were walking swiftly, so that, turn and turn about, they bore their burdens with steady steps, as if they were carried by other men, just like those wandering at their leisure, they went rejoicing.

AJC And so the man of God seeing the supplement of such aid, he encouraged his men to finish the work that they'd started with cheer, and with strong hearts, and to strive to stand together with him in the wilderness; he affirms that it is God's will in this.

AJC And so he restores the church's roof, and the ruins of its walls, and prepares to build anything else that pertains to the needs of a monastery.

AJC At length the blessed Columbanus, having fullfilled his circle of years in the aforesaid monastery of Ebobiensus, after observing a blessed life, restored his soul, released from its limbs, to heaven on 9 Calends of December.

AJC And if anyone wished to know his strenuous activity, he might find it in his utterances.

AJC And his relics are kept safe in this place, where even of mighty works they are properly powerful with our patron Christ, to whom is glory through all the ages of the world. Amen.
