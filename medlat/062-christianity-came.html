HOW CHRISTIANITY CAME TO BRITAIN

Nor should we pass over in silence the story about the blessed Gregory which was carried down to us in the tradition of our ancestors.

They say that on a certain day when, as the traders had recently arrived, many things had been brought together for sale in the market and many people had assembled to buy them, and Gregory himself came with the others, and saw, among other things set for sale, boys of radiant body and graceful mien, and with extraordinary beauty of hair.

And as he gazed on them he asked, so they say, which region or land they had been brought from. And he was told that they were from the island of Britain, whose inhabitants were of such an appearance.

He asked in turn whether these islanders were Christian, or were still entangled in pagan errors. He was told that they were pagans. But he, drawing a sigh from his inmost heart, said, "Alas, what sorrow! that the author of darkness is master of men of such clear faces and that so fair an outside carries a mind free of inward grace."

And so he asked once more what the name of that tribe was. The answer was that they were called Angles. But he said, "Good, for they even have angelic faces and it is fitting for such men to be co-heirs of angels in heaven.

What is the name of that province that these were brought from?" The answer was that the people of that province were called Deiri. But he said, "Good, Deiri; they have been uprooted from wrath and called to the mercy of Christ.

What is the king of that province called?" The answer was that he was called Aelli. But he, making a play on the name, said: "Alleluia, it is meet that the praise of God the creator be sung in those parts."

And he went up to the pontif of Rome and of the apostolic see (for he himself hadn't yet become pontif) and asked him to send some ministers of the word to the tribe of the Angles in Britain, to convert them to Christ; he himself was ready to accomplish this task, with the Lord's help, if it yet pleased the apostolic pope for this to happen.

And while he was not able to complete this, for, although the pontif wished to allow him what he had asked, the citizens of Rome didn't let him leave the city for a long time; then, while he himself was performing the office of the pontificate, he accomplished the task that long had been desired; indeed sending other preachers, but with he himself aiding the preaching with his encouragements and prayers so that it would bear fruit.
