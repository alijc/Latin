THE LAST OF THE MEROVINGIANS

START

AJC The Merovingian tribe, that French kings were wont to appoint themselves from, up to King Hildricus, who by the order of Stephan, the pontiff of Rome, was deposed and shorn and shoved into a monastery, is thought to have endured.

AJC And it is possible in that end that it can seem that not long ago it had no vigor, nor anything in itself besides the plain hollow name of 'king' that it carried.

AJC For both the wealth and the power of the kingdom were held in the hands of the palace prefects, who were called the mayors of the palace, and it extended the summits of authority to them.

AJC And nothing else of the king was left, except to sit on the throne, content with such a royal name, excessive hair, and long beards, and to create the appearance of rule, to give audience to ambassadors coming from everywhich place, and upon their departure to deliver the answers, which he had been told, or even ordered, as if by his own power. When besides the useless name of 'king' and precarious wages of life, which the prefect of the court furnished to him just as it seemed, to possess anothing other than his single villa of very small income, in which house and from which he had servants the supplying him necessities and exhibiting servility of a small multitude.

AJC Wherever he had to go he went by carriage, because he was habitually hauled by a yoke of oxen with a country plowman driving.

AJC Thus to the palace, thus to the public assembly of his people he who was honored every year for the advantage of the kingdom, thus to return home he was wont to go.

AJC But the administration of the kingdom and all which either at home or abroad for doing and disposing was managed by the prefect of the court.
