AJC It also happened that Charles, in his travels, unexpectedly came to a certain coastal city of Narbonese Gaul.

AJC And while he was breakfasting, without notice, at its port, Norman scouts conducted a raid.

AJC And when, after seeing the ships, some said they were Jewish, others African, and even others British merchants, the wise Charles, recognizing from their construction or their speed that they were not merchants but enemies, said: "Those ships aren't filled with merchandise, but teeming with bitter enemies."

AJC When they heard this, with each one eager to outrace the others, they swiftly hurried to their ships.

AJC But in vain.

AJC For when the Normans discovered that he was at that very place, Charles the Hammer, as they were wont to call him, for fear that all their weapons be blunted on him, or destroyed, shattered into tiny pieces, in an unparalleled flight they avoided not only the swords of pursuers but even their eyes.

AJC However, Charles, devout, just and God-fearing, rising from table stood at the east window and for a long time wet with tears beyond all price, since no-one dared challenge him. Finally he spoke, apologizing to his warlike nobles for such demeanor and tears:

AJC "Do you know, my loyal men, why I weep so much?

AJC It's not because I fear that those fools and nobodies could do me any harm; but I'm deeply saddened that they dared touch that shore while I lived, and I'm twisted with a great sorrow, since I foresee such evils will happen to my descendents and their subjects.
