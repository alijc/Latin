A Dialogue

START

AJC Pippin: What is literature? Albinus: The guardian of history.

AJC P: What is a word? A: The herald of the soul.

AJC P: Who produces a word? A: The tongue.

AJC P: What is the tongue? A: A whip for the air.

AJC P: What is air? A: The guardian of life.

AJC P: What is life? A: The joy of the blessed, the sorrow of the wretched, the wait for death.

AJC P: What is death? A: The unavoidable outcome, the uncertain journey, the tears of the living, the testaments of the firmaments, a thief of man.

AJC P: What is a man? A: A possession of death, a passing traveler, a guest of the place.

AJC P: What is a man like? A: An apple (tree).

AJC P: How is a man placed? A: Like a lamp in the wind.

AJC P: Where was he placed? A: Within six walls.

AJC P: Which are? A: Above, below, before, behind, to the right and the left.

AJC P: How many companions does he have? A: Four.

AJC P: Who are they? A: Heat, cold, dryness and wetness.

AJC P: How many in different ways is it? A: Six.

AJC P: What are they? A: Hunger and fullness, rest and toil, wakefulness and sleep.

AJC P: What is sleep? A: An image of death.

AJC P: What is the liberty of a man? A: Innocence.

END
