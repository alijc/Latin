To Reginb...

To my dearest Reg..., Lupus sends greetings in the Lord.

As is fit, we certainly long for your arrival, which certain letters have already promised; but we urge that a prudent route be chosen with a watchful care, because with the uprisings in the realm of our King Charles, robberies are committed with impunity, and nothing is happening more surely or constantly than the violence of robberies.

Therefore, a fellowship of travelers must needs be sought, by whose size and strength a party of robbers is avoided, or, if needs must, is driven off.

I pray that you condescend to bring us Sallust's "Catiline Conspiracy" and "Jugurthine War" and the books "Against Verres" and any others that you are aware of, either that we have that are damaged or that we don't have at all, so that, by your kindness, both the faulty ones may be corrected and the missing ones, that would never be had except through you, obtained with greater pleasure by being unexpected.

I wish you to be healthy and happy.
