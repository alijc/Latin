To Ansbald

Lupus sends greetings to his dear Ansbald.

I have borne it with distress that, with so great an opportunity, as I will say briefly, you had no wish to either send or write anything to me. Nevertheless, although you have put our task aside so easily, for which I blame you, I myself will by no means imitate you, but will, with pious encouragement, embrace the one who scorns me.

I will have the letters of Cicero that you sent compared with our own, so that, if it can be done, the truth will be elicited from both.

But hand Cicero's "On Aratus" over to this our courier, so that the bits that our Eigil found to be wanting might be supplied from what I hope to obtain.

Fare well, and protect me ever in your prayers.
