AJC

To Ansbald

AJC Lupus sends greetings to his dear Ansbald.

AJC I have borne it with distress that, with such a verified opportunity, as I will say sparingly, you wished to neither entrust nor write anything to me.

AJC Nevertheless, although you so easily might have put our task aside, for which I blame you, I myself will by no means imitate you, but will embrace the one who scorns me with pious encouragement.

AJC I will compare the letters of Cicero that you sent with our own, so that from each, if it can be done, the truth will be elicited.

AJC However, hand Cicero's "In Arcto" over to this our courier, so that the bits that our Eigil found to be wanting might be supplied from that which I believe I'm going to obtain.

AJC Fare well, and protect me ever in your prayers.

END
