To Hincmarus

AJC To the most illustrious Bishop Hincmarus, Lupus wishes eternal salvation.

AJC I was afraid to direct Bede's commentary on the apostle from Augustine's works, because the book is so large that it can neither be concealed in one's bosom, nor can it be comfortably contained in a sachel; although, if either happened, one must fear the hostile rapacity of brigands, that the beauty of the codex itself would assuredly arouse, and so perhaps it would be destroyed both for me and for you.

AJC Hence I myself mean to safely lend you the recounted volume as soon as, God willing, we may safely meet somewhere.

AJC Now I have undertaken to send as many pine nuts as the courier can take, which is ten.

AJC Let our same courier, directed for this, as was proposed by you, recieve your reply, as soon as you write it, it freed me of an impediment, so that in reading it I might be either instructed or amused.

AJC May you kindly remember me I wish to fare happily.
