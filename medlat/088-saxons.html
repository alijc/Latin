On the Origin of the Saxon Tribe

AJC Indeed, let me first explain a bit about the origin and condition of these people, following mostly conjecture in this part, as so much time has obscured nearly every certainty.

AJC For there are varying opinions on this, with some judging that the Saxons came from Danes and Normans, but others valuing, as I myself have heard as a young man, a certain declaration from the Greeks, who said that the Saxons were the remnants of a Macedonian army, which, after following Alexander the Great, was scattered all over the world upon his untimely death.

AJC Still, there is no doubt that was an ancient and noble tribe; and an oration about them is entwined both in Agrippa's speech to the Jews and in Josephus, and is demonstrated in the opinion of the poet Lucan.

AJC But we know for certain that the Saxons were carried into these parts on ships and first landed at the spot that's today called Hadolaun.

AJC Indeed, when the inhabitants, who are purported to have been Thuringians, took their coming ill, they took up arms against them; but the Saxons resisted bitterly, and held the port.

AJC Then, after a long period of fighting between them, with many falling on both sides, it pleased them both to discuss a peace, and to enter into a treaty; and the treaty was made on the agreement that the Saxons should be able to to sell and buy supplies. What's more, they'd refrain from plundering fields and slaughtering men. And that treaty stood inviolably for many days.

AJC But when the Saxons ran short of money, not having anything to buy and sell, they decided that the peace was useless for them.
