AJC Gerbert to Emperor Otto

AJC I would prefer to report cheerful matters to the most serene ears of my lord, rather than sad ones.

AJC But when I see my monks become thin with hunger, I am pressed hard by want, and cannot possibly keep silent.

AJC Indeed this evil is bearable, so long as a better hope is not taken away.

AJC I do not know where in the books our leases are listed. everything in the monastery's sanctuary has been sold, the collection money is nowhere to be found, the store-houses and barns are empty, and there's nothing in our purses.

AJC So what am I, sinner that I am, doing?

AJC If the grace of my lord could be done, I'd be better off poor and alone with the Gauls than begging with other needy men with the Italians.

AJC Rainer the Frank knows much about you and is eager to gain your respect, I believe that he has put together a great deal about the state of your power, which will be reported to you, but will neither be entrusted to an envoy or written down on paper, except by your decree.
