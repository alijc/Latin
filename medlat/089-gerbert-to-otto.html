AJC Gerbert to Emperor Otto

I would prefer to report happy tidings to the most serene ears of my lord, rather than sad ones. But when I see my monks grow thin with hunger and pressed by want, how can I possibly keep silent. Indeed this evil would be bearable, if a better hope were not at the same time taken away. I do not know where in the books our leases are listed. everything in the monastery's sanctuary has been sold, the collection money is nowhere to be found, the store-houses and barns are empty, and there's nothing in our purses.

So what am I, sinner that I am, to do?

If the grace of my lord could be done, it'd be better for me alone to be poor among the Gauls than begging with so many other needy men among the Italians.

Rainer the Frank, a close friend, is eager to gain your respect. He has entrusted to my confidence a great deal about the state of your empire. It is to be reported to you, but will neither be entrusted to an envoy or written down on paper, except by your decree.
