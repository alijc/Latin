To Adalberon, the archbishop of Reims

What I could have done concerning your work in Mantua I will explain more in person in speech than in writing from a distance. I don't know which keys I should send for the books because they all got jumbled together.

Pray obtain the history of Julius Caesar from his lordship Adso, the abbot of Montier-en-Der, and copy it for us, so that you may have those that we have for yourselves and you may hope for those that we later found, that is, 8 volumes of Boetius on astrology, also the very famous one on the forms of geometry, and others no less remarkable.

Only your absence disturbs our prosperity, day and night.
