To Hugh from His One-time Teacher Gerbert

My lord has enriched me, according to the breadth of my soul, with the greatest honors. For which part of Italy does not hold on to the blessed Columbanus' property? Indeed this is so due to the largess and benevolence of our Caesar. Truly, Fortune decided otherwise.

AJC Of course, he has honored me, according to the breadth of my soul, with the greatest enemies. For which part of Italy does not hold my enemies?

My powers are unequal to those of Italy.

The condition of peace is this: if I bear being plundered, they won't kill me. If I demand my own, they'll pursue me with swords. When they can't kill me with a sword, they'll assail me with darts of words.

The imperial majesty is scorned, not only in regards to myself but even against its very self in the division of the sanctuary of God. Because I am unwilling to consent to the things done according to the leases, I'm labelled a cruel tyrant. Caesar himself, the most excellent of all men, is regarded as an ass by the scoundrels.

O most faithful of friends, pray you not desert a friend in counsel and aid. Remember what I told you, that I'd prefer to be a soldier in Caesar's camp than a king among strangers.
