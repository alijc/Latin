AJC To the monk Rainard

AJC Do not think, dear brother, that it comes about through my own fault that I an so long away from the presence of my brothers.

AJC After I left you, I went on a series of journeys to carry out the suit of my father Columbanus, seeking resources.

AJC The ambitions of royality, and these dire and unfortunate times have turned right into wrong.

AJC To no man is honesty justly repaid.

AJC Nevertheless, since I know that everything depends on the purpose of God, who simultaneously exchanges the hearts and kingdoms of the sons of men, I patiently await the end of things.

AJC And I advise and encourage you, brother, to do the same.

AJC In the meantime, I have one big ask for you, may it come to you with neither danger nor detriment and bind me to you in friendship as much as is possible.

AJC You know how zealously I collect copies of books from everywhere.

AJC You know how many writers there are in all the cities and lands of Italy.

AJC Therefore go as my sole confidant, and at your own expense cause these to be copied for me: M. Manlius on astrology, Victorius on rhetoric, and Demosthenes' Opthalmicus.

AJC I pledge to you, brother, and pray you to be assured that I will keep this faithful compliance and this praiseworthy obedience under holy silence, and will repay the sum of whatever you pay out according to your receipts, and at whichever time you command.

AJC Signify how much and to whom, both for your services and that we may extend our correspondence, more frequently gladdening us with your letters, and there may be no fear that it comes to anyone's notice what you put down in good faith in our letters.
