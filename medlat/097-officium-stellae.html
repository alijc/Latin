The Service for the Star

Let the first one say: The star glows red with a great brilliance.

Let the second one say: Which points out a child, the king of kings.

The third: Whose coming the prophecies of old have indicated.

The first: Come.

The second: Come.

The third: Come, let us honor him, for this one is the Lord our God.

Then let them join together and say in unison: Therefore let us go and seek him, offering him gifts, gold, frankincense and myrrh, for we know what was written: "All kings will honor him, all peoples will serve him."

The messenger to Herod: Hail, king of the Jews. Lo, wise men come and, led by a star, seek a child, the king of kings.

Herod to the messenger: order them to come before me, so I can know all, Who they are, why they come, by what hearsay do they seek us.

The messenger to the wise men: Royal mandates summon you; do not go half-heartedly.

The wise men to Herod: May the powerful king of the Isrealites live forever.

Herod to the wise men: Whom do you seek, foreigners?

The wise men: We seek a child, the king of the Jews.

Herod: By what sign did you learn who the child-king that you seek is?

The wise men: We learned that this was the child, with a star in the east showing us.

Herod: If you believe that this one reigns, tell us.

The wise men: Acknowledging that he reigns with mysterious gifts, We have come from a far off land to honor him, Revering the triple God with three gifts.

Let the first say: With gold I honor the king.

The second: With frankincense I honor the priest.

The third: With myrrh I honor the mortal.

Herod to the messengers: My colleages, summon here to me the scribes learned in prophetical writings.

The messengers to the scribes: You who are skilled in the law are summoned by the king, hurry up and come with the prophets' books.

The scribes to Herod: Hail, king of the Jews.

Herod: Scribes, you are asked to tell if you have seen anything about this boy written in the books.

The scribes to Herod: We have seen, lord, in the prophets' lines that Christ is born in Bethlehem, the city of David, with Isaiah prophesying thus: "Bethlehem, you will not be least among the chief cities of Judah; for from you will come a leader who should rule my people of Israel, for he will save his people from their sins."

Herod to the wise men: Go and scrupulously investigate the boy, and return and report what you've found to me, so that I too may go and honor him.

The wise men to the shepherds: Shepherds, tell whatever you have seen and announce the birth of Christ.

The shepherds to the wise men: We saw an infant wrapped in cloths and choirs of angels praising the savior.

The wise men: Behold the star that we saw in the east again precedes us brightly, which Balaam had said would rise from a Jewish lineage, which blinds these panicstruck eyes of ours with a flashing light.

The midwives: Who are these men who, led by a star, approach us bearing unheard of things?

The wise men: We whom you see are the kings of Tartessus, Arabia and Saba, bearing gifts to Christ the king the new-born Lord, whom we have come to honor, following a star.

Midwives: Behold, the boy whom you seek is here. Hurry now and honor him, for he is our redemption.

Let the first one say: Hail, God of Gods.

The second: Hail, prince of the ages.

The third: Hail, the life of the dead.

The first: King, accept this gold.

The second: Take this frankincense, you true God.

The third: And this myrrh, the sign of the tomb.

The angel: Everything that was said by the prophets has been fulfilled; so return by another road, lest you be informers of the king's so great vengeance.

I bring a message to you from on high: Christ, the ruler of the world, was born in Bethlehem in Judea, for thus the prophecy had said before.

We praise you, God.
