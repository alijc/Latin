A BURIAL SERVICE

After the mass the priest, wearing an alb without a silk cassock, should come up to the head of the deceased; and two clerics of the second rank standing at the head of the deceased should begin the following antiphon three times, with the choir accompanying all it each time to the end.

Ant. The groans of death enveloped me, the pains of hell enveloped me.

Then after the third repetition this should follow:

Lord have mercy. Christ have mercy, Lord have mercy.

Do not say the 'Our Father', nor 'Lord be with you', nor 'Let us pray', but only the Oratio, with the priest speaking in a restrained voice, that is, without musical notes:

Do not enter into judgment with your servant, Lord, because no man will be justified in your presence, unless he is granted forgiveness for all his sins through you.

Therefore we ask that your judicial sentence not overwhelm him, whom the true supplication of Christian faith commends to you, but with the aid of your grace let him escape the judgment of retribution, he who, while living, was marked with the seal of the holy Trinity.

Through Christ our Lord. Amen.

All of the following prayers are to be said in the same way. Then the cantor should begin the responsory:

You who resuscitated stinking Lazarus from the tomb, Lord, give them rest.

And a place of indulgence.

Give them eternal rest, Lord. And a place.
