A BURIAL SERVICE

After the mass the priest, wearing an alb without a silk cassock, should come up to the head of the deceased; and two clerics of the second rank standing at the head of the deceased should begin the following antiphon three times, with the choir accompanying all it each time to the end.

Ant. The groans of death enveloped me, the pains of hell enveloped me.

Then after the third repetition this should follow:

Lord have mercy. Christ have mercy, Lord have mercy.

Do not say the 'Our Father', nor 'Lord be with you', nor 'Let us pray', but only the Oratio, with the priest speaking in a restrained voice, that is, without musical notes:

Do not enter into judgment with your servant, Lord, because no man will be justified in your presence, unless he is granted forgiveness for all his sins through you.

Therefore we ask that your judicial sentence not overwhelm him, whom the true supplication of Christian faith commends to you, but with the aid of your grace let him escape the judgment of retribution, he who, while living, was marked with the seal of the holy Trinity.

Through Christ our Lord. Amen.

All of the following prayers are to be said in the same way. Then the cantor should begin the responsory:

You who resuscitated stinking Lazarus from the tomb, Lord, give them rest.

And a place of indulgence.

Give them eternal rest, Lord. And a place.

Verse: You who are about to come to judge the living and the dead and the world through fire.

And it should be sung through by the choir with its own verse, and meanwhile the priest should go round the body with the thurible and cense it.

Similarly, the following should be done in the responses:

Then is said: "Lord have mercy. Christ have mercy, Lord have mercy", without the "Our Father" and without "The Lord be with you; but only with "Let us pray".

Prayer. God, for whom all things live, and for whom our bodies, dying, do not perish but are changed for the better, we suppliants entreat you that whatever sins your servant, deceived by the devil and by his own iniquity and frailty, has entered into, contrary to your will, may you, holy and merciful, wash them away with your leniency and command that his soul be accepted, to be led by the hands of holy angels into the bosom of your patriarchs, that is, Abraham, your friend, and Isaac your chosen, and Jacob your beloved, where sorrow and sadness and sighing have fled. And where the souls of the faithful are gladdened by blessed delight, and in the last day of the great trial among your sanctified and your elect may you cause it to secure a portion of your eternal glory, which the eye did not see, and the ear did not hear, and which did not rise up in the heart of man, but which you have prepared for those who love you.

Through Christ.

Resp. Woe is me, Lord, because I have sinned so much in my life; what will I, a miserable sinner, do? Where will I flee, unless to you, my God?

Have mercy on me.

Until you come in the last day.

Verse. My soul is greatly disturbed; but come to its aid, my Lord.

Until you come.

And it should be sung through by the choir with its own verse, and meanwhile the body should be censed as above.

Then should follow: "Lord have mercy. Christ have mercy, Lord have mercy", without the "Our Father", and without "The Lord be with you"; but only with "Let us pray".

Prayer. We pray you, Lord, have mercy on your deceased servant, that he, who kept your will in his prayers, not receive repayment in punishment for his deeds: as the true faith united him in the troops of the faithful here, so may your mercy unite him with angelic choirs there.

Through Christ our Lord.

Then the cantor should begin the response: Free me, Lord, from eternal death on that terrible day.

When the heavens and the earth shall be moved, while you come to judge the world through fire.

Verse. That day, the day of wrath, the day of calamity and misery; the great and very bitter day.

And it should be sung through by the choir with only a single verse, namely "That day" as above, and meanwhile the body should be censed once around by the priest, and afterwards should be sprinkled with holy water.

Then should follow: "Lord have mercy. Christ have mercy, Lord have mercy",

Here the priest should ask the attendees to pray for the deceased, saying: For the soul of N. and for the souls of all the faithful deceased.

Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be thy name; thy kingdom come; thy will be done, on earth as it is in heaven.

Give us this day our daily bread; and discharge our debts as we discharge our debtors,

And lead us not into temptation. But deliver us from evil.

Do not enter into judgment upon your servant, Lord: for each living man will not be justified in your sight.

From the gate of hell: Lord, pluck up their souls.

I trust to see the good deeds of the Lord: In the land of the living.

Lord, hear my prayer: And let my crycome to thee.

The Lord be with you: And with your spirit.

Let us pray.

Prayer. Lord, bend your ear to our prayers, in which we supplicants entreat your mercy, that you place the soul of your slave, which you ordered to depart from this world, in a region of peace and light, and that you command that he be a fellow of your saints. Through our Lord Jesus Christ.

Here the body should be carried to the grave, while the cantor begins:

Ant. May the angels lead you into paradise, may the martyrs receive you in your coming, and may they guide you into the holy city of Jerusalem.

PSALM. 1. In the departure of Israel from Egypt, the house of Jacob from the barbarian nation:

2. Judea became his sanctuary, Israel his power.

3. The sea saw, and fled: the Jordan was turned back.

4. The mountains leapt about like rams: and the hills like lambs.

5. Why is it, sea, that you fled: and you, Jordan, why did you turn back?

6. Mountains, why did you leap about like lambs, and you hills like lambs.

7. The earth was moved by the presence of the Lord, by the presence of the God of Jacob.

8. Who turned a stone into pools of water, and a rock into springs of water.

1. Not to us, Lord, not to us: but to your name give glory.

2. For your mercy, and your truth: may the nations never say: Where is their God?

3. But our God is in heaven: everything that he willed, he has done.

4. The nations' images are silver and gold, the work of human hands.

5. They have a mouth, and will not speak: they have eyes, and will not see.

6. They have ears, and will not hear: they have nostrils, and will not smell.

7. They have hands, and will not stroke: they have feet, and will not walk: they will not cry out in their throats.

8. May they become like those who do these things: and all who trust in them.

9. The house of Israel trusted in the Lord: he is their aid and protector.

10. The house of Aaron trusted in the Lord: he is their aid and protector.

Those who fear the Lord trusted in the Lord: he is their aid and protector.

12. The Lord did not forget us: and he blessed us: He blessed the house of Israel: he blessed the house of Aaron.

13. He blessed all who fear the Lord, the insignificant with the great.

14. May the Lord add to you: to you, and to your sons.

15. You are blessed by the Lord who made heaven and earth.

16. Heaven, the heavens are for the Lord: but he gave the earth to the sons of men.

17. The dead will not praise you, Lord: nor will any who descend into the lower regions.

18. But we who are alive, we bless the Lord, from this day now and forever on.
