The Conversion of Lanfranc

START

AJC There was a certain great man, by the name of Lanfranc, who was born in Italy and restored good Latin style to its ancient status of knowledge, is recognized, with all due love and honor, as its greatest master; likewise Greece, the master of nations in liberal studies, freely heard and respected his students.

AJC He was born in the city of Pavia; his parents, citizens of the same city, were held as great and honorable among their fellow-citizens.

AJC For, as it is said, his father was of the class of those who administered the laws and statutes of the city.

AJC At a young age Lanfranc lost his father, whom he should have succeeded in honor and dignity. He went on to university, abandoning his city for the love of learning.

AJC He remained there for a long time, and returned with basic knowledge in every secular subject.

AJC From there he left his homeland and crossed the Alps and came to Gaul, in the time of Henry, king of the Franks, and the famed duke of the Normans, William, who conquered England for himself with arms.

AJC And passing through France, bringing as many scholars of great account with him as possible, he arrived in Normandy and, staying in the city of Avranches, taught there for a time.

AJC Indeed, the most knowledgeable man considering that to attempt to gain mortal favor is foolishness, and since, except for He who made the universe, who is eternal, and those who serve him, all are bound for destruction, he turned his mind and zeal to obtaining His love.

AJC Therefore, he took the perfection that he found in literature and set about using it as a plan for salvation and for pleasing God, so that, abandoning everything, even the law itself, he might follow the One who said: "If any man wishes to come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross and follow me."
