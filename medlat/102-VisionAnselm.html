The Vision of Anselm

Now when their son Anselm was a small boy, he happily stretched his mind with conversations with his mother, as much as his age permitted.

And when he heard that God on high in heaven solely guided and maintained everything, he supposed, like a boy raised in the mountains, that heaven, where the palace of God was, was supported by the mountains, that he could get to it through the mountains.

And after he rolled this thought about in his mind for a while, it happened that one night he had a vision, that he should climb the mountain peak and hasten to the palace of the great king, God.

Indeed before he got to the foot of the mountain and began to climb, and was still on the plain, he saw women, servants of the king, reaping grain, but they did this overly heedlessly and indolently.

And the boy, noticing and disapproving of their idleness, proposed in his mind that he must accuse them before the Lord the king.

Then he climbed the mountain and entered the royal palace, and found God alone with his steward.

For since it was autumn, it seemed a good idea to him to send his household to harvest the crops.

And so the boy went in and was summoned by the Lord.

AJC He came near and sat at his feet.

He was questioned with pleasant courtesy who he was and from where, and what he wished.

He replied to the questions as well as he knew how to.

Then at God's command shining white bread was brought to him by the steward and he restored himself with it in God's presence.

And so in the morning when he recalled what he had seen before his eyes, he believed, as a simple and innocent boy, that he really had been in heaven and had been restored by God's bread, and he asserted this publicly before others.

Then the boy grew up and was loved by all.

For he had an honest character which made him greatly loved.

He is given over to his letters, he learns, and accomplishes a great deal in a short time.
