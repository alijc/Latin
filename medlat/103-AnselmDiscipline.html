AJC And so, once when a certain abbot, who was held as exceedingly devout, was speaking with him about women who were religious monastics, and they were conversing about the children raised in the monastery, he added: "What, I pray, is to be done about them?"

AJC They have been subverted and are incorrigible, we don't leave off beating them, day and night, and they always become worse than before."

AJC At which Anselm was amazed: "You don't leave off," he said, "beating them?

AJC And when they have grown up, what sort are they?"

AJC "Stupid," he said, "and brutish."

AJC But he said: "With what profit do you spend your substance?

AJC You rear beasts from men."

AJC "And we," he said, "what can we do then?

AJC We restrain them in every way so they can succeed and we accomplish nothing."

AJC "You restrain them?

AJC Tell me, I ask, lord abbot, if you plant a sapling in your garden and then confine it in every way thus, so that it can't extend its branches at all, when you liberate it years later, what sort of tree would issue from this?

AJC Surely a useless one, with crooked and tangled branches.

AJC And who would be at blame for this except for you, who immoderately confined it?

AJC Surely this is what you do with your children; they were planted in the church's garden as an oblate, to grow and bear fruit for God.

AJC But you so alarmingly restrict them with threats and beatings on every side so that, deep down, they are not able to master their own liberty.

AJC And so, unwisely suppressed, they amass opinions among themselves that are crooked and tangled as if with thorns; they maintain, nourish, and foster these with such strength for nourishment as to obstinately evade anything that could be applied to correcting these opinions.

AJC From this, because they sense in you no love, no tenderness, no kindness, or sweetness for themselves, it results that they won't credit you for any good afterwards, but will believe that all your actions against them come from hatred and spite.

AJC And it wretchedly happens that, as one by one they grow in body, so hatred and suspicion of every evil grows in them, forever crooked and prone to sin.

AJC And since they have truly been nourished with charity towards no-one, they deign to look at no-one except with glowering brows or a side-eye.
