On Humility

START

AJC But the first part of education is humility. There are many examples of this, especially these three that pertain to the reader.

AJC First, that he hold no knowledge, no writing, as worthless; second, that he not be ashamed to learn from anyone; third, that after he has gained knowledge, he not hold others in contempt.

AJC This deceives many men, because they wish to seem wise before their time.

AJC For it makes them break out in a swelling of pride, as they now start to pretend to be what they are not and to feel shame for what they are, and so slide further from wisdom because they wish not to be wise, but to be thought wise.

AJC I have known many this sort who, although they yet lack the first principles, condescend to take part in nothing except the highest points, and from this alone they think that they have become great if they have read writings or heard speeches of those greater and wiser.

AJC "We," they say, "saw those men, we have read from them.

AJC They often used to speak to us.

AJC Those very great men, those famous ones, they recognized us."

AJC But would that no one would recgonize me and I would know everything.

AJC You boast that you have seen Plato, not that you have understood him; I think it beneath you to listen to me after that.

AJC I am not Plato, nor do I deserve to see Plato; it's enough for you that you have drunk from that fount of philosophy, but would that you were still thirsty.

AJC After the golden bowl, the king drinks from an earthenware vessel.

AJC What are you ashamed of?

AJC You have heard Plato, and you will hear Chrysippus.

AJC In the proverb it is said: "Perhaps an ass knows what you do not."

AJC There is no man to whom it was given to know everything, nor in turn did it ever befall anyone to learn anything of the spirit from nature.
