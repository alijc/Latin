AJC We are compelled, albeit unwillingly, to consider the justice of God and the volatility of the world.

AJC For look, as I have said before, at the way of heaven, that turns from east to west, so with time we see that worldly things and the powers of things revolve.

AJC And lest we suppose that mortal matters are found firm in any part of the world, there yet they suffer weakness and in the manner of feverishness, as you have in Job: "They turn night into day and after darkness I again look for light," wherever they may turn we observe that they have sorrrow and toil but have learned nothing of stability.

AJC Doesn't it seem to you to move, as I said, that the position of the feverishness of the world is rolled this way and that?

AJC For feverishness I hope toby restful in alternative of situation they put and therefore toby repeated revolutions when they are turned they disturb themselves here and there.

AJC Thus too much temporal power from Babylon having rolled down to the Medes, from there to the Persions, after that to the Greeks, and ultimately to the Romans and under the Roman name was transferred to the French.

AJC Where/when while/until, so to speak, it had molded a firm seat, it might seem gonna remain, so many toby evil (as in higher ones if lies open) it began to lie underneath so that deservedly with Job one might say: "As I come to the day, worn out be nocturnal tortures, I await the night, which while/until I master, impaired by greater sorrow, again I long for the day to come."
