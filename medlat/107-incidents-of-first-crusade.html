AJC And so, with the victory, by God the Creator, won, the commander of the Babylonian army, whom in their native tongue they call an emir, was perplexed and lamented greatly, unable to admire the misfortune that had happened to him.

AJC He thought hard on the limitless supplies that he had brought, the youth, selected for their strength and appearance, his outstanding arms, his soldiers' courage and, as I should have said, all his horse and, what was wont to bear the greatest security for the courage of weak men, and that they fought in their own province, before the gates of their city, which was a sure refuge.

AJC But he considered the French, with an army inferior in every way, with their youth overcome long lasting hunger, with rusty swords and black lances, poor armor with soldiers devoid of strength, with any who seemed more distinguished than the rest numbed by bitter poverty, with their mounts exhausted with hardship of every kind; and, to come to brief end, he was amazed that the most worthless men of all, an exile band, had overcome his countless native sons; more, in fact, that through the most downcast of men they had destroyed the glory of the entire East.

AJC It also aided our victory that, when the retreat was signaled in the enemy army and the emir in charge of Ascalon saw the Babylonian commander turn tail, he ordered that all those fleeing be kept out of his own city.

AJC The enemy were clearly exceedingly amazed that the French had not chosen to fight before the walls of Jerusalem, as if on account of the neighboring garrison, but had advanced against themselves in a march of nearly two days.
