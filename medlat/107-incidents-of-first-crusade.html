AJC And so, with the victory, by God the Creator, won, the commander of the Babylonian army, whom in their native tongue they call the admiravisus, was troubled, and as the misfortune that had happened to him was not powerful enough to wonder at, lamented greatly.

AJC He thought hard on the limitless troops that he had led, the youth, selected for their strength and appearance, his outstanding arms, his soldiers' courage and, as I have thus said, all his horse and, since he was wont to bear the greatest security for the souls of the weak, and that he had fought in his own province, before the gates of the his city, which was a sure refuge.

AJC But he considered the French in every way with an inferior army, with their youth overcome long lasting hunger, with rusty swords and black lances, poor armor with soldiers devoid of strength, with any who seemed more distinguished than the rest numbed by bitter poverty, with horses exhausted with hardship of every kind; and, so that I might remove a brief end, the poorest men of all, an exile, a globe, he was amazed that they'd overcome the countless native-born; more, in fact, that through the most downcast of men they had destroyed the glory of the entire East.

AJC It also aided our men's victory the more that, when the flight was signaled within the enemy army, when the admiravisus who controlled Ascalon saw the Babylonian commander turn tail, he ordered that all the fugitives be kept away from his own city.

AJC The enemy were clearly amazed still the same exceedingly that the French had not at all chosen to fight before the walls of Jerusalem, as if on account of the neighboring garrison, but had advanced against themselves in a march of nearly two days.
