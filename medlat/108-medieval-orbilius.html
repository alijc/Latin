A Medieval Orbilius

AJC I was beaten once in school; but the school was nothing more than the dining room of our house.

AJC For the teacher put aside his concerns for the other students that he had accepted, for my sake alone.

AJC For by thus increasing his profit and conferring the honor of her patronage my prudent mother had exacted this from him.

AJC And so, when released from those studies in the evening, I approached the maternal knee reluctantly, beaten yet again beyond my due.

AJC And when she'd begin to ask, as was her wont, if I'd been beaten that day, and I, lest I seem to accuse the teacher, would utterly deny it, she'd, willy-nilly, throw back my undergarment, what they call a tunic, no indeed, a shirt, and take heed of the livid little ribs of my small back from blows of a switch and my skin, covered all over with welts.

AJC And after she'd grieved, from the bottom of her heart, for the punishment inflicted too savagely for my tender years, disturbed and with burning eyes and welling with grief, she said: "After this you'll never become a scholar, nor will you pay such great a price to learn your letters."

AJC At this I looked at her as reproachfully as I could and said: "Even if I were to die, I'll not stop learning my letters and will become a scholar."

AJC For she had promised that if I wanted to become a knight, she'd give me the equipment and arms, after I'd risen to the age for it.
