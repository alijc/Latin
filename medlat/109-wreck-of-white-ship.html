The Wreck of the White Ship

Thomas FitzStephen approached the king and said to him, offering him a gold mark: "Stephen FitzAirardus was my father, and he served your father on the sea his whole life.

For he conducted him to England, borne on his own ship, when he went to England to wage war against Harold.

Now he pleased the king in his attendence in this manner until his death, and, honored by him with many rewards, he prospered splendidly among his own countrymen.

This same privilege, lord king, I ask of you and have fitted out the vessel that is rightly called the White Ship exceeding well for royal service."

And the king said to him: "What you ask pleases me.

Indeed, I have chosen an apt ship for myself, which I'll not exchange; but I now entrust to you my sons, Willian and Richard, whom I love as myself, with the great nobility of my kingdom."

When the sailors heard this they rejoiced and, flattering the king's son, asked him for wine for drinking toasts.

By the king's order many barons boarded the ship with his sons and nearly three hundred, as I suppose, were on the ill-fated ship.

AJC Accordingly, two novice monks and Count Stephen with two knights and several others left, because they observed that such a large crowd of wanton and insolent youths were on board.

AJC For there were fifty skilled oarsmen and armed marines there, who had already taken seats on the ship and, swollen with insolence and dead drunk, scarcely acknowledged anyone with respect.

AJC Alas, how many of them had minds empty of pious devotion towards God "who governs the vastness of the sea and the fury of the air."

AJC And due to this they drove away the priests who had come there to bless them, and the other attendants who were carrying the holy water, mocking them with shame and guffaws; but a little later they received retribution for their derision.

AJC It was only men, along with the king's treasure and vessels full of wine, that filled Thomas's ship, and they pressed him hard to chase the royal fleet, which was already plowing the seas.

AJC Indeed, Thomas, out of his head with drink, was confident in his strength and that of his companions, and rashly promised to get ahead of all those who had already sailed.

AJC Finally, he gave the signal to sail.

AJC In turn the sailors, not at all half-heartedly, seized the oars and joyfully, since they knew not what lay before them, adjusted the other rigging and with great vigor made the ship speed across the sea.
