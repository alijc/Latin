Abelard in Retirement

And so I conveyed myself to a certain lonely place in the district of Troyes, that was already familiar to me, and there, with the agreement of the bishop of the country, I constructed, on land that some men had given me, an oratory in name of the Holy Trinity, out of reeds and straw at first.

AJC And hiding there with a cleric of mine, I could rightly recite this to the Lord: "Behold, I left in flight and remained in the wilderness."

And when scholars learned about this, they began to throng together from every direction, leaving cities and castles to dwell in solitude - to build small huts for themselves instead of great houses, to subsist on rustic vegetables and coarse bread instead of dainty fare, to lie down on straw bedding instead of soft beds, and to build tables of turf.

And indeed you'd've thought that they imitated earlier philosophers, about whom Jerome too, in his second book against Jovian, wrote these words: "The entrance of vice to the soul is via the senses, as if through windows.

The city and the citadel of the mind cannot be taken, unless a hostile army charges through its gates."
