He Compares Their (I.E. Cluny's) Extravagance with the Parsimony of Monks of Old

START

AJC In the beginning, when the monastic order began, who could believe that monks would arrive at such ignorance.

AJC Oh how much we differ from those who became monks in the days of Anthony!

AJC Accordingly, when they visited each other in charity as the opportunity arose, they gained the bread of life from each other with such passion that they thoroughly forgot about food for the body, and often passed a whole day with their bellies fasting but not their minds.

AJC And this was a proper order, since the past was treated with deference by the more worthy part; this highest discrimination, the more it assumed the greater it was; finally, this true charity, where the souls that Christ died for in charity were revived with such solicitude.

AJC "But when we assemble together," if I might use the words of the apostle, "it is not to eat the lord's supper."

AJC Indeed there is no-one who'd seek heavenly bread, no-one who'd bestow it.

AJC Nothing about scriptures, nothing about salvation is done for souls; but trifles and laughter and words are pushed out into the wind.

AJC As much as our gullets eat at banquets, that much are our ears fed on rumors; in which you, closely attentive, might not know the manner in eating.

AJC But meanwhile the food trays are set down; and for only meat, from which one is abstained, great bodies of fish are served in double portions.

AJC And since you were sated with he first portion, if you touch what follows, it'll seem to you that you never tasted what came first.
