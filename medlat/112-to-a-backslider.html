TO A BACKSLIDER

I grieve for you, my son Gaufridus, I grieve for you.

And deservedly so.

For who would not lament that the flower of your youth, that, with the angels rejoicing, you had offered, unsullied, to God in an aroma of sweetness, is now trampled by demons, and polluted by the filth of sin and the squalor of the world?

How could you, who were called by God, follow the devil that's calling you back, and you, who began to follow after Christ, suddenly draw his foot back from the very threshold of glory?

In you I now prove the truth of the word of the Lord, which declared: "The enemies of a man are those of his own household."

Your friends and those nearest you have advanced and have stood against you.

They called you back into the lion's jaws and put you again in the gates of death.

They put you in darkness, like the dead of the world, to have you descend into the stomach of hell; already it hastens to swallow you and to deliver you as food to be devoured by roaring beasts.

Turn back, I beg, turn back, before the abyss swallows you, and the pit presses its mouth down upon you; before you sink further than you can rise; before you are thrown into the outer darkness, bound hand and foot, where there is weeping and gnashing of teeth; before you are thrust into a dark and hidden place in the gloom of death.

Perhaps you are ashamed to return because you yielded for a season.

Be ashamed at your flight and not that, after fleeing, you return to the battle and fight again.

But the battle is not over, the battle lines have not yet marched away from each other; yet victory is at hand.

If you are willing, we refuse to conquer without you and do not begrudge you your portion of glory.

We'll meet you joyfully, we'll take you in with glad embraces and will say: "It is meet to feast and rejoice, for this our son had died, and lives again; he was lost, and is found."
