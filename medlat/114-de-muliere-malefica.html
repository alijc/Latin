AJC In these days it happened like this in England, not through a heavenly miracle but through an infernal deception; because, as I will report, the credit of the story will not falter, even though the minds of those who hear it may not believe.

AJC I myself heard it from such a man who swore that he saw it, whom I would blush to not believe.

AJC A woman was staying in Berkeley, accustomed to wrongs, as later revealed, not ignorant of ancient auguries, a patroness of the gullet, a mistress of wantonness, not putting the manner with disgraces, who might not yet have been old, but was dancing ever so close to old age.

AJC One day, when this woman was dining, a crow, that she kept as a pet, cried out more loudly than usual I know not what; and when she heard this, the lady's knife fell from her hand, and also her face turned pale; and she said with a prolonged moan, "Today, my plow has reached to the furthest furrow: today I will hear and will take great harm."

AJC Upon this utterance a messenger of miseries entered; he was affected and hesitant as to how he might approach, and he said, "I bring word to you from that village" (and named the place) "your sons have died and all of your family has perished from a sudden catastrophe."

AJC The woman at once lay down, wounded to her soul with this grief; and sensing that illness was creeping over her vitals, with persistent letters summoned her surviving children, a monk and a monk.

AJC When they arrived she spoke to them in a sobbing voice: "Sons, I have always attended to demonic arts through some wretched fate of mine; I was the dregs of all sins, the teacher of allurements.

AJC Yet there was, among these evils, hope for your sanctity, which might comfort my wretched soul; desparing for myself, I was depending on you; I proposed you as champions ranged against demons, defenders facing fierce enemies.
