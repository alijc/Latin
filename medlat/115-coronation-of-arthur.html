The Coronation of Arthur

Finally, when everyone had assembled in the city, with the rite at hand, the archbishops were summoned to the palace to crown the king with the royal diadem.

And so Dubricius, since the court was held in his diocese, ready to perform the ritual, undertook this task for the king.

After some time, the king, dressed in his royal robes, was elaborately conducted to the church of the metropolitan's see; for on his left and his right, two archbishops supported him.

Now four kings, namely, of Scotland and Cornwall, Demetia and Venedocia, whose right it was, preceded him, bearing four golden swords.

Likewise, an choir of those who had taken holy orders was performing wonderful songs.

While in another part archbishops and bishops conducted the queen, adorned with her own ensigns, to the church of maidens dedicated to God.

Likewise the four queens of the four aforesaid kings carried four white doves before her, as was the custom.

And the women who attended followed her with the greatest joy.

Then, when the procession was over, so many kinds of music, so many songs arose in both churches that the soldiers who were there were overwhelmed with the sweetness and couldn't decide which of the churches they should make for.

And so, they swarmed now to this one, now to that; and although the whole day had been given to the celebration, they felt not a bit of weariness.

At last, after divine services were celebrated in both churches, the king and queen took off their crowns and put on lighter trappings and walked to the feast, he to his palace with the men, she to another with the women.
