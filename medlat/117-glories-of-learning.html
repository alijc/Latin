The Glories of Learning

START

AJC The reward of books is most pleasant, not only in the many but also in each one specifically since, with all the disadvantages of intervening time and space removed, they in turn furnish the presence of friends to one, and do not allow what is worthy of understanding to be destroyed or neglected.

AJC For too, the arts would have perished, the laws vanished, the offices of faith and of all religion broken down and even the use of proper eloquence would have failed, had not divine mercy taken care for the use of books to remedy human infirmity in mortals.

AJC Many examples of things that are the incentives and encouragements of worth would rouse or protect nothing at all, had not, through this same care, the pious solicitude of scribes and the conqueror of idleness been transmitted to future generations.

AJC Accordingly a short life, a dull sense, a torpor for negligence, a useless occupation permit us to understand very little, and these same things are continually cast out and torn away from the mind by the swindler of knowledge, the ever hostile and faithless stepmother of memory, forgetfulness.

AJC For who would know Alexanders or Caesars, who would admire the Stoics or the Peripatetics, if the records of scribes didn't mark them?

AJC Who would accept and follow the footsteps of the apostles and prophets, had not the holy epistles had consecrated them to posterity?

AJC Only then do triumphal arches advance illustrious men to glory as the engraved inscription teaches us for what reasons and whose.

AJC Then, in the end, the spectator recognizes the liberator of the country, the founder of peace, when the inscription proclaims the conqueror, whom our Britain gave birth to, Constantine.
