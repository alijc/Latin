Alexander and the Pirate

The strength of all philosophy is patience; since even Socrates not only said that a wise man could not take offence but could, in the face of all fortune, remain unmoved through the strength of his virtue.

And lest you believe that patience is so pleasing to only philosophers, the examples of the emperors themselves agree in showing its excellence.

In Greece who was greater or more famous than Alexander?

His teacher Antigonus broke his lyre and threw it away, saying: "You're now of a fitting age to reign, and it would be shameful in a king to be ruled by a pleasure of luxury."

And he bore this most patiently, even though he was usually very impatient, and just as he surpassed his father in virtue so did he in vices.

It's also written that a captured pirate gave Alexander an elegant and truthful answer.

AJC For when Alexander asked why he seemed to hold the sea in turmoil, he spoke with frank defiance: "For the same reason as you do the world.

But since I do it with a single ship, I am called a thief; since you do it with a great fleet, you're called emperor.

If Alexander were alone and captive, he'd be a thief; if the nations served at Dionides' nod, Dionides would be an emperor.

Indeed the cases do not differ at root, except that he is worse who seizes more wickedly, he who forsakes justice is baser, and he who attacks the laws is more plainly guilty.

For I flee the laws, while you fight them; I honor them, however I can, and you despise them.

The unfairness of fate and the straits of my fortune make me a thief, insufferable pride and insatiable greed make you one.

If fortune were to grow gentle, I'd perhaps become better; but when you grow more fortunate, you'll be worse.

Alexander, amazed at the courage of the man challenging him, and rightly so, said: "I'll test whether you'd be better, and I'll change your fortune, so that you won't fall short in it unless it is ascribed to your character."

And so he ordered that he be enrolled in the army so that he could thereafter serve under sound laws as a soldier.
