AJC TO MY DEAREST LORD AND TO HIS FRIEND R., ARCHDEACON OF NANTES, P. OF BLOIS. SALVATION AND IF ANYTHING IS BETTER THAN SALVATION

AJC The day before yesterday you entrusted two of your nephews to me for instruction; one still of a boy's years, and the other fully into puberty.

AJC But now through your letters you greatly praise and entrust a character of greater and assert that you have never found a man of a finer vein; for that reason you pray and urge that his tutor show an earlier vigilance concerning him; for you gently judge that the finishing touches are applied to him, the construction of which is due to another's effort.

AJC But yet, the matter is turned on its head.

AJC For I have better hopes for the education of the one who came to me coarse and unformed than for the other one, whose veins have already partly hardened, as if it stamped the image of a different teacher on him.

AJC Clay and wax and other things of the sort offer themselves for the shaping and stamping, and are more easily and faithfully fashioned to the judgment of the artist if nothing previously undertook the outlines of forms in them.

AJC Indeed, Quintilianus reported in his book "On the instruction of orators" a certain Timothy, celebrated in the art of flutes, was accustomed to charge double fees from those whom someone else had taught.

AJC For the work is double in these: once in washing out the less commendable education that had been given, and again in presenting the knowlege that produces profit and fame; for scarcely is it unlearned what a younger age learned, since "Once a fresh jug has been dipped, it will hold the odor for a long time."
