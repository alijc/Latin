
Sicelides Musae, paulo maiora canamus.&
non omnis arbusta iuvant humilesque myricae;&
si canimus silvas, silvae sint consule dignae.&

Ultima Cumaei venit iam carminis aetas;&
magnus ab integro saeclorum nascitur ordo. &              5
iam redit et Virgo, redeunt Saturnia regna,&
iam nova progenies caelo demittitur alto.&
tu modo nascenti puero, quo ferrea primum&
desinet ac toto surget gens aurea mundo,&
casta fave Lucina; tuus iam regnat Apollo.  &             10

Teque adeo decus hoc aevi, te consule, inibit,&
Pollio, et incipient magni procedere menses;&
te duce, si qua manent sceleris vestigia nostri,&
inrita perpetua solvent formidine terras.


Sicilian Muses, let's sing a little more.
Not all orchards please or lowly tamarisks either;
if we celebrate the woods, may they be worthy of a consul.

From Cumae??? now comes the latest age of song;
from the untouched(?) of generations a great order springs.
Now returns Virgo, and the kingdoms of Saturn,
now a new race from high heaven is sent down.
You, in the manner of a newborn boy, by whom an iron nation
will first end and throughout the world a golden one rise up
you chaste honeycomb Lucina; your Apollo now reigns.

And >you precisely splendor this of time, >you to/by consul he will undertake,
Pollio, and the great months will begin to advance;
>you to/by leader, if any traces are left of our crime,
by useless everlasting alarm they will release the lands.
