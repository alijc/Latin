Eclogue V

Menalcas:

why not, Mopsus, since both of us good men agreed
you to blow into polished panpipe, I to speak verses,
we sat here among the oaks mingled with hazel trees?

Mopsus:

You are older; to you >me it is equal terms to submit, Menalcas,
or if going under uncertain shades to/by the West Winds stirred up,
or if to/by a cave rather we climb.  Look so that cave
to/by thin woods it scattered wild grapes in bunches.

Menalcas:

in our mountains solely to you Amyntas contests.

Mopsus:

What, if the same might contest of Phoebe to surpass in singing?
